# WAVE 1 — GENRE-LANE & COMMERCIAL POSITIONING

*Decides whether this is even the right book. If it reads literary, downstream
craft scores are moot. Run all four in parallel, isolated context.*

---

## Agent 1.1 — Genre-Lane Auditor

**Mandate:** Does the text keep the cozy-LitRPG / progression-fantasy promises *as
a binding contract*?

Score these dimensions (1–10, per rubric):
- **Stakes calibration** — cozy = real threat to *home / found-family / way of
  life*, never existential grimdark, never toothless. (The basilisk truly
  petrifies; no creature the reader loves dies.)
- **Stat-block as contract** — visible stats, light math, nothing that wins a beat
  appears out of unlock-sequence. System boxes read as a fair contract, not décor.
- **Cozy floor / growth ceiling** — comfort is the floor, capability the ceiling.
- **Promise integrity** — the stated non-negotiables (System never rewards
  killing; safe place endures; care + cleverness over violence) are honored on
  the page, not just asserted.

**Watch-fors (deductions):** grimdark intrusion that breaks safe-place trust;
out-of-sequence unlocks; system crunch so heavy it stops being cozy, or so absent
it stops being LitRPG.

**Verdict it owns:** *True to lane / drifting literary / drifting grimdark.*

---

## Agent 1.2 — Comp & Market Positioner

**Mandate:** Triangulate against the stated comps — *Beware of Chicken* (tone &
found-family), *Legends & Lattes* (cozy competence, low stakes), *The Newt and
Demon* (care + base-building + KU length), *Demon World Boba Shop* (scary
aesthetic reframed as comfort).

Score:
- **Shelf placement clarity** — would a reader who loved the comps recognize this
  in 2 pages?
- **Breakout differentiator** — the ONE thing this does that the comps don't
  (the care-not-killing System + bureaucracy-as-antagonist). Is it on the page early?
- **Cover/blurb-able hook** — is there a line/premise that sells itself?

**Watch-fors:** in-lane but generic (competent comp-clone, no differentiator);
differentiator buried past the opening.

**Verdict it owns:** *Breakout hook present / generic in-lane.*

---

## Agent 1.3 — Commercial-vs-Literary Discriminator

**Mandate:** The adversary to pretension. Flag literary-fiction *tells* that repel
the KU / Royal Road reader.

Score (higher = more commercial / healthier):
- **Forward motion over interiority** — does introspection stall the plot, or ride on top of it?
- **Payoff posture** — are rewards *delivered*, or withheld as "theme"?
- **Prose service** — does the prose serve the read, or admire itself (ambiguity,
  lyricism-for-its-own-sake, deliberate opacity)?
- **Clarity of want** — is the protagonist's concrete goal legible every scene?

**Watch-fors:** ambiguity-as-virtue, unresolved-on-purpose, mood over momentum,
sentences that ask to be underlined instead of read through.

**Verdict it owns:** *Commercial / contaminated by literary instinct.*
(Returns a hard binary token: `COMMERCIAL` or `LITERARY-CONTAMINATED`.)

---

## Agent 1.4 — Read-Through / Retention Strategist

**Mandate:** Chapter-level dopamine and the binge contract.

Score:
- **Cookie-per-chapter** — does each chapter pay at least one of: number-up,
  mystery deepened / clue, relationship beat, landed joke, competence-display?
- **Hook-out / warm-out** — does each chapter end pulling the reader forward
  (tension) or settling them in to return (comfort)? Map each ending.
- **Finish-compulsion** — name the single thing making a reader finish Book 1.
- **Next-appetite** — name the single new hunger that opens Book 2.
- **Promise Ledger integrity** — seeds planted vs. payoffs visible in these chapters.

**Watch-fors:** a chapter that pays no cookie; cliffhanger cruelty (cozy readers
punish betrayal); plateau with no live clock or mystery.

**Verdict it owns:** *Binge-engineered / episodic-pleasant.*
