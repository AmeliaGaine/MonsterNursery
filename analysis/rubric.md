# THE MONSTER NURSERY — BREAKOUT-COMMERCIAL ANALYSIS RUBRIC

*Shared scoring contract for every agent in the swarm. Read this before scoring.*

## What we are testing

The single decision: **Is this cozy-LitRPG breakout-grade commercial genre fiction
— true to its lane, engineered for read-through, delightful — and NOT literary
fiction wearing a genre coat?**

## Scoring scale (anchored — use these anchors, not vibes)

Every dimension is scored **1–10**:

- **1–3 — Disqualifying.** Actively works against the lane / breaks the reader's
  trust / would cause a KU or Royal Road reader to bounce.
- **4–5 — Competent but invisible.** Does no harm; also gives no one a reason to
  tell a friend. This is the "merely publishable" band.
- **6–7 — Strong in-lane.** A reader who already likes the subgenre is satisfied.
- **8–9 — Breakout signal.** The thing that makes a reader screenshot a line,
  leave a review, or open the next chapter at 2am.
- **10 — Top-tier.** Best-in-class for the subgenre; a comp other authors cite.

**Breakout threshold = 8.** "Solid in lane" lives at 6–7. Be stingy: most prose
is a 5. Reserve 8+ for evidence you can quote.

## Evidence rule (non-negotiable)

Every score must cite **at least two line-level quotes** (with the chapter and a
short quoted phrase) as evidence. A score with no quote is rejected by the
Evidence Adjudicator and does not count. Note misses as well as hits.

## Two-pass reading

1. **Blind pass** — read once for *experience only*. Record a gut 1–10 "delight"
   number before any analysis. Breakout fiction must win the blind read.
2. **Forensic pass** — re-read for evidence, line-cite, score each dimension.

Report **both** numbers. A big gap (blind high, forensic low — or vice versa) is
itself a finding.

## Output format every agent returns

```
## <Agent name>
**Blind delight:** X/10
**Verdict:** <one-line call in this agent's own terms>

| Dimension | Score | Evidence (quote + ch) |
|---|---|---|
| ... | X/10 | "…" (Ch1) ; "…" (Ch2) |

**Top strength:** <one, quoted>
**Highest-leverage fix:** <one specific, actionable change>
**Breakout / Solid-in-lane / Below-lane:** <pick one>
```

## Verdict aggregation (Synthesizer only)

A manuscript is called **BREAKOUT-COMMERCIAL** only if ALL hold:

1. **Wave 1 (positioning)** — Genre-Lane Auditor AND Read-Through Strategist both ≥ 7,
   and the Commercial-vs-Literary Discriminator returns "commercial."
2. **Wave 2 (craft)** — at least **5 of 7** craft agents score their headline
   dimension ≥ 8, and **none** below 5.
3. **Wave 3 (adversarial)** — the Red-Team Skeptic lands **no structural kill**
   (a kill = a guardrail breach that breaks reader trust, not a fixable nitpick).

Otherwise: **SOLID-IN-LANE** (publishable, retention-safe, not yet breakout) or
**MISPOSITIONED** (drifting literary/grimdark — fix lane before craft).
