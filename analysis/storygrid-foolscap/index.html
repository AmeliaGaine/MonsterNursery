The three things that matter most

1 · Two outlines exist — and the prose follows the older one

The repo holds a top-level outline (basilisk, griffin, dragon; Ep3 “Inventory of Ruin”) and an evolved build/OUTLINE.draft.md v2 (Kraken, Cockatrice, no dragon; Ep3 “Why Her?”; adds the Crane romance). Chapters 1–3 as drafted follow the older one. Lock one canon before drafting further.

2 · Guns already fired that v2 never pays off

Chapter 1 plants, in print: “Basilisks must wear soft blinkers during story hour,” the habitats Feather Run & Quiet Den, and a “weather vane shaped like a tiny dragon.” v2 has no basilisk, griffin, or dragon. These are promises to the reader that the current design breaks.

3 · The thesis is never tested — so the climax can't truly be lost

“Dangerous creatures are children” is the book's whole argument, but the guardrails forbid any creature ever harming anyone on-page. The global value at stake has no scene where the negative threatens to win. In Story Grid terms the “All Is Lost” beat is muted and the core event reads as foregone. The fix is one reversible, non-gory near-catastrophe averted by care — not by the genre contract. The dragon is the perfect vehicle, which is why we recommend restoring it (see the slideshow).

Priority order

  1. Lock one canon. Until this is decided, nothing else is safe. We recommend restoring the dragon and grafting v2's legal/grief spine onto it.
  2. Resolve the planted guns — restore the creatures, or consciously scrub the Ch1 plants. No drift.
  3. Engineer real jeopardy once — a near-catastrophe averted by care, and one genuine cost in the Arc-4 crisis.
  4. Put Pike on-page at the climax — the genre's core confrontation can't be won against a document.
  5. Decide the romance's altitude — commit Crane with early beats, or frame him as a Book-2 seed.
  6. Restore a public set-piece for the Arc-2 climax, de-narrate the three big reveals, and clear the six live continuity items.

Do the first four and the book crosses from “trap-free skeleton” to “obligatory scenes complete.” Do all of them and it is, by our assessment, masterclass-locked at the outline layer — the only remaining variable is prose execution, which the drafted chapters already prove is in hand.

The Five Commandments — global & per-act

Story Grid holds that the Inciting Incident → Progressive Complications/Turning Point → Crisis → Climax → Resolution must fire at every level: the global story, each act, and each scene. The book's act architecture is impressively deliberate. The gaps are concentrated in the Crisis (no true dilemma) and the Climax (antagonist absent; thesis untested).
