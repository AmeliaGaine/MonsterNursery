# MACRO-DOORS.md — Stage 3.1 (Macro-Loop Architect)

*The book modeled as four nested levels of door, each on the Promise / Progress /
Payoff spine. Derived verbatim from `ARCHITECTURE.md` (IMMUTABLE) and obeying
`CANON.md` + all six `GUARDRAILS.md`. A door's PROMISE is what it swears the moment
it opens; PROGRESS markers are the visible mid-points that prove movement (anti-
plateau, G5); the PAYOFF is where and how it lands. Per G6, every door below the
series level **closes its own promise in full AND opens a bigger one** — full
satisfaction + "the world just got bigger" — never a cruel cliffhanger. Episode
numbers are the locked recommendation (49 eps · 4 arcs 12/12/13/12).*

## PROSE WALKTHROUGH — HOW THE DOORS NEST

The book is built as a matryoshka of promises: the largest door opens first and closes
last (or never), and each smaller door opens later and closes earlier — so the reader
always *feels* the plot tightening as inner dolls snap shut in reverse order.

**The series door opens first and stays ajar.** On Ep1 the System greets Liora as if it
were expecting her — a custodian-key turning in a lock no one knew was there. That is
`L-SERIES`: the question of *who finally inherits the will a dead woman wrote into the
rules.* It is the one door deliberately left open at the end of Book 1, so the reader
finishes fully fed yet carrying exactly one bright unanswered question. It is named
(Ep47) but not paid until Book 2, when Pike files for custodian transfer (Ep49).

**Inside it sits the book door, the finish-compulsion.** `L0` opens the same Ep1 and is
the engine that drives all 49 episodes: *will Briarwick be recognized as real before the
apparatus shuts it down — and who was Edda?* Its outer half is a legitimacy climb up the
Renown ladder (Stranger → Regional), each rung contested by the fear-economy's next
instrument; its inner half is the parasocial pull of falling for Edda in installments.
Both halves are wired to climax on the same late episodes (Archive Ep31, terminal Ep46,
voice breaks Ep47), so neither is decorative — the reader cannot resolve the home without
meeting the ghost. The book door closes dead last, at Ep49.

**Within the book door, the four arc doors form the spine's vertebrae — and they are the
gear-train.** Each arc door delivers *full satisfaction* and then opens a strictly bigger
one, never a cruel cliffhanger (G6), because the *upkeep* a win creates becomes the next
arc's foreground problem. Arc 1 proves care works on one creature and Liora becomes a
carer — which makes the home *visible* (a target), so Arc 2 must build a pipeline and win
the town. Being loved locally *triggers an audit* (Pike lands), so Arc 3 must dig up *why
care was outlawed* — and the answer (the fear-economy was engineered; Rhea was erased) is
itself the bigger threat, because the designer now moves to seize. So Arc 4 spends
everything earned defending the home, wins by care + cleverness, and lands recognition —
which reveals the will can be *inherited*, swinging the series door wide for Book 2. The
arc-outs breathe in the mandated rhythm: warm → soft-hook → warm-with-ache →
warm-climax-then-door.

**At the innermost layer, the chapter doors keep the whole nest turning.** Every episode
closes a loop it opened (a cookie paid) and cracks one slightly larger — so at any moment
the reader holds 2–4 live loops at different scales: a chapter-loop closing *now*, an
arc-loop (inspection / send-off / Archive) still open, and the book-loop (recognition +
Edda) open above both. Closing the small one *feels* like a stopping point, but the medium
and large just got bigger. The natural place to stop is always one episode further than
where the reader is — until Ep49, when every loop is closed LIFO save the one ajar door.

**Consistency check against ARCHITECTURE.md arc climaxes.** The four arc-door payoffs map
exactly onto the locked arc climaxes in §2E.4: Arc 1 = "The First Inspection" (~Ep12,
warm-out); Arc 2 = "The First Send-Off and the Petition" (~Ep24, soft-hook); Arc 3 = "The
Archive and the East Wing" (~Ep36, warm-with-ache); Arc 4 = "The Seizure and the
Recognition" (~Ep46–49, warm-climax-then-door). Each arc-door payoff doubles as the next
arc's hook per the §2B.6 gear-train, and the close order obeys the §2D.2 LIFO matryoshka
(`L-SERIES` depth 0 ajar → `L0` depth 1 closes Ep49 → depth-2 spine loops Ep47–48 →
deeper dolls earlier). G1–G6 hold: every threat is aimed at home/way-of-life and
metabolized into warmth, no loved creature is lost, and the one cliffhanger is an
*appetite*, not a fall.
