## KILL 1 (RT2 1.1) — "Why her?" tautology / un-sensable clearance condition → **CLOSED**

**Original kill:** the System is dormant until Liora presses the stamp, so recognition
"cannot be *triggered by* the act of stopping" — there is "no sensor for whether the
holder quit culling." "Why her" collapses to "she had the key."

**The fix's load-bearing move:** the System was never off — it was **passive-RECORDING**.
The **Cull Ledger** has logged every Control clerk's cull/spare choice under Briarwick's
jurisdiction since the purge. Edda's will arms one activation condition keyed to that
ledger: reactivate for the first key-holder who *refuses a cull and files a sanctuary
hold in its place.* This directly answers the original objection — the machine does not
have to be awake to sense the refusal, because the *sensor* (the Ledger) was running the
whole time and the green-stamp is the logged event.

**Is it seeded, or asserted?** Genuinely seeded, four points, escalating:

- **Ep1 (seed):** the green EMERGENCY SANCTUARY HOLD over the red CULL is rendered as the
  trigger. New System box: `> Cull Ledger: refusal logged — key-holder. (1 of 1.) Note:
  this ledger has been recording. You are the first to file a hold over a cull.
  Reactivation condition met.` Crucially, the scene states the ledger "has been
  *recording*" — i.e., the sensor predates the activation, which is exactly what the
  original kill said was missing.
- **Ep3 (echo):** `"Cull Ledger — continuity recognized. Prior entry, same ledger:
  [REDACTED] Previous Custodian. Status: heir."` The redacted custodian sits in the SAME
  ledger — the reader sees the mechanism early, not just at payoff.
- **Ep31 (mechanism reveal):** the restored Archive shows the Ledger is Edda's instrument;
  Sorrel reads the two ruled columns (CULL/SPARE) decades deep; the flyleaf activation
  clause is read aloud; Liora's own name appears in the last entry marked SPARE / "Refusal
  #1 — key-holder." The cold "click" of the sensor is delivered here, as mechanism, with
  the emotional payoff deferred.
- **Ep47 (payoff):** the screenshot reveal makes it literal: `"clearance condition =
  recipient deed 'stops culling,' detected via the Cull Ledger… The key opened nothing on
  its own; the recorded refusal woke the System."` The episode explicitly retires the old
  tautology in its own voice: *"Not 'she held the key' — holding the key was never enough;
  clerks had held keys before. She chose, on the record."*

**Adversarial test:** the fix does not merely re-label the old "the System just knows." It
supplies a concrete recording instrument, shows it logging on Ep1, shows the reader the
column on Ep3, explains the function on Ep31 (via a human, Sorrel, who can read it), and
pays it on Ep47. The original "the machine is off so it can't sense" objection is met head-
on by "passive-recording, not dead." Contracts amended (ARCH §530–534, CANON §100–101).
**Verdict: CLOSED.** The mystery now mechanically works: there is a rendered sensor, the
refusal is a logged event, and "why her" resolves to "the Ledger caught the one choice the
will was built to wait for," not "she inherited the key."

*Minor residual (not a kill):* the device leans hard on the "Edda pre-laid everything"
foresight (RT2 4.2) — the Ledger is one more thing the dying clerk arranged. This is a
tone/convenience note, not a logic hole; the sensor itself is now real.

## KILL 2 (RT2 3.1) — Ep45 climax win invented / paperwork-magic → **CLOSED**

**Original kill:** "completing a seizure form truthfully *inverts* it into a recognition
certificate" is a new rule introduced at the moment it's needed; the loophole-reading
motif sets up "void the order," not "the order is secretly a recognition application."

**The fix's move:** the winning instrument is no longer "complete the form into its
opposite." It is the **SUPERSESSION DOCTRINE** — a higher-order municipal form supersedes
a lower one; the 1842 founding charter is the highest instrument in the county. The
seizure is outranked, not inverted.

**Is it seeded three times before it pays?** Yes, and the plants are real small wins, not
mentions:

- **Ep6 (plant 1):** a deficiency/enclosure-code notice is beaten by filing a superseding
  higher-order form; System states the doctrine outright: `"Doctrine: a higher-order form
  supersedes a lower one. The founding charter (1842) is the highest instrument in this
  county."` Flagged "First of three plants (Ep6 / Ep8 / Ep17)."
- **Ep8 (plant 2):** she stacks a 1842 charter clause over a structural-condemnation
  Control notice; `"Doctrine holds: charter supersedes Control instrument."`
- **Ep17 (plant 3):** a deficiency notice on her unpermitted ward is superseded by the
  charter's care-works clause; `"a higher form outranks a lower one… Filed: 2nd
  supersession (cf. Ep6). You are getting faster at this."` (and she "reaches for it
  before she reaches for the argument").
- **Ep45 (payoff):** the seizure is a Monster Control instrument (lower); the charter
  (highest in county) is filed OVER it; the order is *outranked*, not struck. The scene
  is explicit and self-aware: `"the doctrine doesn't care how big the lower form is, only
  that it's lower."` The recognition (Compliance→100%) and the seizure-break are now
  separated across episodes as RT2 recommended — Ep45 BREAKS the seizure via supersession;
  the formal RECOGNITION landing is deferred to Ep49 (where it is the formal record of
  work already certified @43/44/45, explicitly "not a re-won fight").

**Adversarial test:** the decisive move at Ep45 is the exact tool planted three times
earlier; it is the same verb (supersede) applied to a larger lower-form. This is the
textbook fix for an invented climax — plant the tool, use it small, pay it big. The two-
wins-merged problem RT2 flagged (break ≠ affirmative recognition) is also resolved by
splitting the beats: Ep45 = break, Ep49 = recognition. **Verdict: CLOSED.** No new rule
is introduced at the climax; the win is a planted instrument. Contract amended (ARCH
§535–537).

## KILL 3 (RT2 2.1) — Pike's motive self-contradictory (destroyer vs. custodian-thief) → **CLOSED**

**Original kill:** Book 1 Pike tries to seize/condemn/raze (Ep44 clause 9 "dissolve the
wards"); Book 2 Pike wants to *become custodian*. "You don't bulldoze the house you're
filing to inherit." No coherent want across the book.

**The fix's move:** Pike is a consistent **ACQUIRER**. He never wanted Briarwick razed;
he wants the care-economy privatized under Control — seize-to-relicense / custodian-
transfer. Every Book-1 move is a takeover tactic.

**Is it consistent across Ep24/38/39/49?** Yes, and the scenes are now explicit about it:

- **Ep24:** Pike's audit is framed as **acquisition due-diligence** — "chain-of-title,"
  "valuation, not violation-search," "He does not intend to close this premises. He
  intends to acquire it." A higher compliance score reads as a higher *appraisal* — the
  leash and the price tag are the same line.
- **Ep38:** the seizure order is now explicitly a **custodian-transfer-to-Control / re-
  license**, NOT a demolition: `"It contains… no clause that ends with the premises gone…
  The disposition clause does not condemn the house; it re-licenses it… He is acquiring
  it: custody to Control, asset re-issued under licence, fee schedule appended."` This is
  the single most important correction — the original kill cited Ep44/clause-9 wreckage;
  the order is now an acquisition wearing a seizure's letterhead.
- **Ep39:** Pike's first move is a **planted code-change designed to devalue toward a
  forced/distress transfer** — "you don't condemn a property you mean to keep; you
  devalue it… fail the house on paper, depress the asset, then offer to re-license it."
  Continuous acquisition logic.
- **Ep49:** he **files for custodian transfer** — "cannot cull a recognized nursery… so it
  files to TAKE the one it can no longer touch." Tagged A3 as "his CONTINUOUS acquisition
  want (seize→take, never raze), escalated."

**Adversarial test — does the Book-1 want now match the Book-2 hook?** Yes. Book-1 want
(seize-to-relicense / devalue-to-transfer) and Book-2 hook (custodian transfer) are now
the same want at escalating altitude. The "why raze the asset you want to inherit"
contradiction is gone because nothing in Book 1 razes anything — the wards-dissolving
demolition framing has been removed and replaced with re-licensing. **Verdict: CLOSED.**
Contracts amended (ARCH §538–540, CANON §102–103).

*Residual nuance (not a kill):* RT2 2.2's question — why Pike personally *waits* through
Arcs 2–4 instead of erasing Liora the way he erased Rhea — is softened by the new "recognized
= un-erasable; erasure only works on the deniable" logic threaded through Ep24/49, but it is
never stated as crisply as a single Pike-constraint line. That was a [MAJOR], not a [KILL];
it is improved, not airtight.

Original direct contradiction: "dormant until Liora" (Ep1) vs "running warm the whole
time, never let go cold" (Ep30/46). The fix reconciles both with one word: **"dormant" =
passive-recording, not cold** (CANON §101; FIX-DESIGN A1). The Ledger was *recording*

own new box now says the ledger "has been recording," and Ep47 says "the System was not
dead; it was passive-recording." The two statements are now one mechanism. **CLOSED.**

## LOGIC CRACK B — comprehension-free vs. Hearthglow climax (RT2 3.2) → **CLOSED**

Original incoherence: Ep46/47 say "comprehension is free," but Ep45 says the legal close
*empties the jar* — so why does closing the order cost Hearthglow? The fix re-bases the
Ep45 win on **Compliance + Advocacy + the charter**, and is explicit that the Hearthglow

cost), and the legal/comprehension act is consistently free. The two rules no longer
collide. **CLOSED.** (FIX-DESIGN A3 final bullet names this fix explicitly.)
