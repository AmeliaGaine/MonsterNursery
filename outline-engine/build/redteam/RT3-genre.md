## VERDICT (one line)
The progression spine is genuinely engineered and the cozy floor holds (no CANON breach found) — **but the back half lets the WHODUNIT/grief-mystery overtake the LitRPG progression as the felt spine, the prose-plans drift LITERARY (interiority-that-stalls, the "VOICE-GUARD" tic that quotes the purple line then prints it anyway), and the LitRPG dopamine thins to near-zero across Arc 4.** Solid-in-lane with a mispositioned, slow, mystery-heavy second half that risks the binge.

**Tags used:** `[KILL]` = trust-breaking lane/commercial failure · `[MAJOR]` = fixable but loses readers · `[MINOR]` = polish.

## 1. LANE DRIFT

### [MAJOR] The mystery spine overtakes the progression spine in Arcs 3–4 (cozy-MYSTERY drift)
The brief is explicit: *the progression system must stay the spine; the mystery is a thread inside it.* In Arcs 1–2 this holds. From **Ep30 onward it inverts.** Evidence:
- **Ep34 ("Rhea"), Ep37 ("One Organism"), Ep40, Ep46, Ep47** are *all* mystery/lore-excavation episodes whose cookies and turns are revelations (whodunit: who was the prior custodian / what is the System / why did it wake). The stat lines in these episodes are explicitly *frozen*: Ep34 "**no bite, no foreground earn this episode**"; Ep37 "**no Hearthglow spent … the win is the body, not the currency**"; Ep47 "**comprehension is free … Hearthglow untouched at 3 … no win from the reserve.**" The LitRPG ledger goes dormant precisely as the mystery climaxes.
- The book's stated **"one finish-compulsion"** (OUTLINE.draft.md, "read-through engine") is *"who is the dead woman whose love built the System"* — a **mystery question**, not a progression question. The progression ("recognized as real") is wired as the *outer* clock, but the inner/emotional clock the whole structure privileges is the Edda whodunit. For a cozy-LitRPG positioned against Beware of Chicken / Newt and Demon, the spine the reader is *meant to be pulled by* should be the build. Here, by design, it's the grief-mystery.
- **Net:** This is not a CANON breach (the System still rewards care, not killing), but it is **lane drift toward cozy-mystery-with-LitRPG-garnish** in the half of the book where retention is hardest to hold. A Newt-and-Demon reader who came for the base-building gets an archive-excavation novel in Arc 3–4.

### [MAJOR] LITERARY contamination on the page-plans: the "VOICE-GUARD" device is contamination cosplaying as a guard
The scene files repeatedly do this move (Ep24 S4, Ep34 S4, Ep37 S3+S4, Ep47 S3+S4, Ep49 S2+S4): they **write out the full lyrical/literary sentence** ("the name hung in the gold air like a door opening onto a room she'd been living in all along"; "love itself was the crime … Rhea's name a candle the dark had tried and failed to put out"; "the falling star wrote its brief bright wish across the heavens above the home she had ransomed from the cold ledger of the world") **and then say "resist this, write the dry version instead."** GUARDRAILS G2 fails on *"a lyrical sentence that asks to be underlined."* These episodes generate the underline-bait sentence on purpose, every time, at every climax. The *intent* is anti-literary, but:
1. The drafter is handed the purple version first and at length — a contamination risk at the draft stage.
2. The sheer **density of interiority** around each turn (Ep24 S3 is a single ~400-word paragraph of Liora's inner state about being "alone in the middle of being loved") is exactly the "introspection that stalls plot" G2 names as a FAIL. The guard's own existence proves the temptation is structural, not incidental.
**This is the load-bearing finding for the `LITERARY-CONTAMINATED` token.** The Seam Report self-certifies "No `LITERARY-CONTAMINATED` drift at any seam — PASS," but it only checks *seams*, not the *interiors* of the climax episodes, which are where the drift lives.

### [MINOR] Interiority-that-stalls in the ASMR beats (Ep7, Ep35, Ep40)
The three dedicated "low-plot ASMR" beats (Ep7 S2–S3, Ep35, Ep40) foreground texture with the clock "live underneath." Ep7 S3 is a ~250-word single paragraph of sensation with the plot explicitly paused ("She just stops working and feels the house be warm"). This is *inside* the cozy contract (Legends & Lattes has these), but three of them, all structured identically (slow soak + buried clock), risks the "merely publishable" 4–5 band the rubric warns against. Not a breach; a flatness risk.

### Grimdark floor: HOLDS (no breach found)
- **The cull-economy** (Ep1 "CULL. / Stamp. / CULL.", ninety-three culls): rendered as bureaucratic horror aimed *outward at the system*, metabolized into Liora's defection. No on-page killing of a loved creature. PASS G1.
- **Rhea's erasure / Pike:** Pike is "reasonable, not cackling" (Ep24 explicitly), a *filed form*, never on-page cruelty (Ep49: "Pike never appears on-page as a person — his move is a FILED FORM"). Rhea's erasure is bureaucratic deletion, "a wound, not a trophy," no gore. This is dark-but-cozy-floored, correctly. PASS G1.
- **One watch-item:** Ep37's "the apparatus deletes its losers / success schedules deletion / the warmth is the mark" is the *darkest* thematic note in the book and it lands at the Arc-3 climax. It stays inside heat ≤4 (no creature threatened), but it is tonally the closest approach to grimdark fatalism. It is saved by being immediately metabolized into family warmth. Verdict: in-bounds, but it is the single beat a cozy reader could find heavier than the comp promises. `[MINOR]` flag for tone-watch.

## 2. PROGRESSION DOPAMINE (the LitRPG actually satisfying?)

### [MAJOR] Arc 4 is a LitRPG dead zone — the dopamine drains exactly when a finale should spike it
The "every gain books upkeep" cost-rule (dial 5, G3) is honored *rigorously* — arguably **too** rigorously for the finale. Across Arc 4:
- **No new rooms, no new arrivals** (CANON Arc-4 rule) — by design.
- **Hearthglow spends to the floor (3) and stays there** through Eps 45–49; the finale "**wins nothing new**" (Ep49: "the FINALE WINS NOTHING NEW"); recognition is "the formal landing of work already done."
- Eps 46/47/49 explicitly state **"comprehension is free," "no win from the reserve," "no new ability."**
The result: the **last quarter of a LitRPG has almost no stat-ups, no unlocks, no new bestiary, no new mechanics.** Compliance climbs 66→100 at Ep49, but that's a single legitimacy number landing, not a build. For the comps — where finales tend to *spike* the numbers (the big synergy payoff, the capstone unlock) — this is **counter-genre.** The emotional finale (Edda, Matron, recognized) is strong; the *LitRPG* finale is a balance sheet being reconciled. A progression reader feels the engine turn off.

### [MINOR] System-crunch dial 5 is honored — mostly soft-leaning, not hard
Dial 5 (2–4 boxes/ep, light math, specific-behaviour rewards). Spot-check confirms it holds: Ep1 has the [CARE EVENT COMPLETE] delta block (Bond 3→18%, Comfort 0→1, etc.); Ep18 has a genuine triage with real arithmetic ("reserve funds two of three"). **No spreadsheet drift.** But several late episodes lean *soft* — the System boxes become long fond editorial monologues (Ep47's boxes are paragraphs of Edda's voice, not stat lines). The crunch is front-loaded (Arcs 1–2) and dissolves into lore-voice (Arcs 3–4). Net: dial 5 honored in aggregate; trends soft late. Acceptable but watch the Arc-4 boxes for "where did my numbers go."

### Hits (credit where due)
- **Ep18 "What Grows in the Larder"** is the best LitRPG episode: a real Hearthglow triage (fund 2 of 3 needs), an unlock with a genuine cost (RC II you can't afford to deploy), a contained-not-killed resource, all paying a mystery seam. This is the dial-5 cozy-LitRPG done right. **More of Ep18, fewer of Ep37.**
- The **bestiary/collectible drip** (F4: +1 at Ep1, 2, 9, 13×3, 22, 26; Archive Codex at 31, 34) is a correct retention layer — but note it **stops after Ep34.** Arcs 4 has zero collectible hits. (See §3.)

## 3. READ-THROUGH / BINGE (each episode pays a cookie and pulls forward?)

### [MAJOR] The collectible/number cadence dies in Arc 4 — a retention layer goes dark
Per F4, the visible-collection drip (bestiary, codex) runs Ep1→34 and then **stops entirely.** Arc 4 (Ep38–49, the finale arc) has **no bestiary +1, no codex +1, no new room, no new recipe except Ep44 cocoa.** The cookie law (G4, ≥1/chapter) is still met — but the cookies in Arc 4 are overwhelmingly *relationship* and *mystery*, not *number-up* or *competence-with-a-new-toy*. The binge engine in a LitRPG is partly the dopamine of accumulation; that accumulation flatlines for the final 12 episodes. **The book gets more literary and less gamified exactly as it should be hardest to put down.**

### [MINOR] "Chapter that advances nothing" — Ep32 was flagged and patched, correctly; but the patch is thin
The Seam Report's own F5 admits Ep32 ("Security Duet") had a plateau problem and bolted on a "Hearthglow-rebuild clock" tick. The fix works on paper (a clock is now live) but the episode's actual *content* (two creatures learning to take turns) is low-stakes. It's the weakest pull-forward in the book. Not a kill — the guard caught it — but evidence the middle sags without scaffolding.

### Hits
- **Ep1 ends on a genuine page-turn** ("We are going to need a bigger form" + the cracking egg arming Ep2). Strong soft-hook.
- The **alternating warm/hook out-cadence** (G6) is real and well-tracked; no two cruel hooks adjacent; the LIFO loop-close order is genuinely satisfying as a "tightening knot." This is the structural strength of the book.

## 4. COZY PROMISES (CANON non-negotiables on the page)

All five checked against spot-checked scenes. **No breach found.**
1. *System never rewards killing* — PASS. Hearthglow is earned only by care-acts; Ep1's whole premise is the defection from culling.
2. *Nothing loved dies* — PASS. Fosters are *placed* (Slime Ep20, Kettle Ep41), never lost; Rhea/Edda are dead *before* the book and are ghosts/mystery, not on-page loved-creature deaths.

## 5. COMPS / POSITIONING (does it deliver what the comp reader wants?)

### Hook-on-the-page: PASS, early and strong
- **Beware of Chicken** (cozy voice, deadpan, found-family-of-creatures): the voice fingerprint nails it — "Mm." Bertram, the preening stove, "Disagree.", "We are going to need a bigger form." This is the book's strongest commercial asset and it's live from Ep1 page 1.

### [MAJOR] Positioning risk: the book is sold as cozy-LitRPG but reads as cozy-LitRPG → cozy-mystery
A reader who buys for the Newt-and-Demon promise gets a strong 24-episode base-builder and then a 25-episode pivot into an archive-mystery / grief-novel about a dead woman's will. The Edda reveal is *good writing* — but it is not the genre the cover sells. **The comp reader's appetite is strongest for "what do I build/unlock next," and the back half answers "who was Edda" instead.** This is the central commercial weakness: the lane is *correct on the shelf* but *drifts in the second half toward a different (adjacent) shelf.*

## 6. COMMERCIAL WEAK SPOTS (KU / Royal Road bounce risks)

### [MAJOR] Slow/over-written scene-plans (Royal Road sample-bounce risk)
The scene files run to enormous length and **interiority density** at every turn. Ep24 S3, Ep34 S3–S4, Ep37 S3, Ep47 S2–S3 each contain multi-hundred-word single-paragraph interior monologues. If the *prose* renders these at the density the plans specify, individual chapters will feel **slow and ruminative** — the #1 Royal Road early-chapter bounce cause. The OUTLINE word target is a healthy 2,000–2,800/ep, but the *beat density* asks the drafter to spend a third of several chapters inside Liora's head. **The plans over-specify interiority; the drafter needs license to cut it by half.**

### [MAJOR] Arc 4 binge-risk: emotional payoff without progression payoff
Combine §2 + §3: the final arc is mystery-and-grief-resolution with the LitRPG engine idling. For a *cozy* reader this may be fine (the warmth lands); for a *LitRPG* reader (the half of the audience the "LitRPG" label recruits) the finale gives no build, no unlock, no number to chase. **The two halves of the target audience are served unequally by the finale.** Highest-leverage commercial fix: inject *one* earned capstone unlock or a real new-mechanic payoff into Eps 43–49 so the progression reader gets a finale spike alongside the emotional one.

### [MINOR] The mystery may be over-explained (anti-binge in the other direction)
Ep47 is a 5-scene, ~2,500-word episode that *fully* explicates the Edda/Rhea/heir/will mechanism, with the System restating the answer in long fond paragraphs *and* the prose restating it *and* the sequel restating it. G2 says "deliver the payoff" (good) — but this over-delivers; the reveal is explained three times in one chapter. Tighten or the binge stalls on exposition.

### [MINOR] Opening is strong but the charter/loophole mechanic is dense
Ep1 S2–S3 leans on "the 1842 Briarwick emergency-custodianship charter clause" — a bureaucratic loophole that's thematically perfect but is a small cognitive-load bump in the critical first-chapter window. Minor; the egg-hook recovers it.
