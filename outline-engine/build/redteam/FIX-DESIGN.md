## THEME A — make the mystery mechanically work

### A1 — "Why her?" gets a real, seeded sensor: THE CULL LEDGER *(fixes K1)*
The Sanctuary System was never *dead* — it was **passive-recording**. Briarwick keeps a
**Cull Ledger**: since the purge it has silently logged every Monster Control clerk who
processed a creature under Briarwick's old jurisdiction and **what they chose** — cull or
spare. Edda's will encoded ONE activation condition: *the System reactivates for the
first clerk who, holding the founding-charter key, refuses a cull and files a sanctuary
hold in its place.* 
- **The trigger is now a concrete on-page event:** Ep1, when Liora stamps the green
  **EMERGENCY SANCTUARY HOLD** over the red CULL, the Ledger logs *Refusal #1 — key-holder*
  and the System wakes. She is **detected, not assumed**.
- **Seed:** Ep1 add a System line — `Cull Ledger: 1,► refusal logged (key-holder).` ·
  Ep3 the "[REDACTED] Previous Custodian" entry sits in the *same* Ledger (so the reader
  sees the mechanism early). · Ep30/31 the Archive reveals the Ledger is Edda's
  instrument. · **Payoff Ep47:** "why her" = *because the Ledger recorded the one act the
  System was built to wait for.* Not "she held the key" — she **chose**, on the record.
- This also fixes the dormant-vs-warm crack: "dormant" = passive-recording, not cold.

### A2 — seed the climax win: THE SUPERSESSION DOCTRINE *(fixes K2)*
Plant a municipal-forms hierarchy in Arc 1: **a higher-order form supersedes a lower one,
and the 1842 founding charter is the highest instrument in the county.** Liora, the
loophole-lover, learns and *uses* this early (small wins), so the Ep45 climax is a
*planted tool*, not paperwork magic.
- **Seed:** Ep6 (deficiency notice) — Liora beats one code-violation by filing the
  superseding form; the System notes the doctrine. · Ep8/Ep17 — echo (she stacks
  charter clauses over Control notices). · **Payoff Ep45:** the seizure order is a
  Control instrument; the founding charter **supersedes** it, and she answers the seizure
  with the one form it cannot outrank. Set up three times before it pays.

### A3 — fix Pike's want: ACQUISITION, NOT DESTRUCTION *(fixes K3)*
Pike never wanted Briarwick *razed* — he wants the care-economy **privatized under
Control**: a for-profit sanctuary monopoly he licenses. Every Book-1 move is an
**acquisition/transfer** tactic, continuous with the Book-2 hook.
- The Ep38 order is a **custodian-transfer-to-Control** (seize → re-license), not a
  demolition. Ep24/Ep39 Pike's moves are takeover maneuvers (audits to devalue, a
  code-change to force a distress sale). Ep49 "files for custodian transfer" is the
  *same* want, escalated. He covets the asset; he never tried to destroy it.
- Fixes the "comprehension is free" crack too: comprehension costs no Hearthglow, but the
  climax is won on **Compliance + Advocacy + the charter** (Ep45), not on "understanding."

## THEME B — roster & send-off integrity *(fixes K4, K5)*

### B1 — the Phoenix: it CHOOSES to stay *(fixes K4 — and turns the bug into a beat)*
The Pocket Phoenix is **not** placed — it is the one foster that bonds too deep to send.
Make this explicit and emotional:
- Ep19 closes Phoenix's arc as **"placement-ready — declines placement"** (the anxious
  bird re-lights *only* at Briarwick; every trial placement fails because it
  self-combusts with strangers). It becomes the first **foster→permanent** resident.
- **Ep41 boast corrected:** not "every foster placed" but *"three fosters placed and
  thriving; one foster home, found right here."* The pipeline is honest AND the Phoenix
  earns a found-family beat. (It also gives the permanent core a 6th member for Arc-4
  chaos — helps Theme C/D.)

### B2 — reconcile Cinder & Kettle Arc-4 presence *(fixes K5)*
- **Cinder:** matched & departs **Ep33** (parting carried to Book 2 as ache). Remove him
  from ALL Arc-4 rosters and from ep-45 (the on-page contradiction). STATSPINE: freeze
  Cinder's bond at the Ep33 value; no Arc-4 Cinder ticks.
- **Kettle:** departs **Ep41**. Remove the Ep45 "reanimation." Any Arc-4 alarm/shriek
  function transfers to the **Corehound alarm-head** (permanent), which is already the
  synergy partner.
- FRICTION-SCHEDULE + STATSPINE + scenes 33/41/45 all updated to one consistent roster.

  a dead zone. This also gives Ep49 a *progression* spike alongside the wonder set-piece.
