# RELATIONSHIPS & HUMAN CAST — expansion (recast)

*Answers: too-many-antagonists, more found-family humans, Stardew relationship stats,
and the adventurer patrons. Romance = Dr. Sorrel, light sapphic slow-burn (the
breakout-commercial choice for the cozy-fantasy lane). Contracts amended; scenes propagate.*

## 1 — ANTAGONIST REDUCTION (3 → 1)

The antagonist is the **fear-economy**, not people. Only **Pike** is a persistent
human adversary (distant; Book-2). Reframe the other two as the cozy "grump-who-softens":
- **Grindle** — wary obstacle → **late-converting ally**. His arc pays when he *chooses*
  (the withheld signature, Ep43) and, by the finale, quietly roots for Briarwick.
- **Inspector Quill** — already converting → **recurring ally/friend**. By Book 1's end
  she's a quiet protector, not an inspector.
→ Net: **1 antagonist (Pike); +2 humans into the warm cast.**

## 3 — NPC RAPPORT (Stardew-style relationship stats)

A new tracked stat mirroring creature **Bond Trust**, for named humans:
- **Rapport %** (0–100), heart-tiers: **Stranger (0) → Acquaintance (20) → Friend (40)
  → Confidant (60) → Dear (80+).**
- **Grows via:** shared scenes, **gifts (the menu/recipes become gift items — Mood-Teas,
  Storm-Day Cocoa, etc.)**, helping with their problem, and time. (Same care-as-mechanic
  logic as creatures — relationship is *behaviour-earned*, never free.)
- **Heart-event unlocks:** each tier threshold opens a deeper scene — a backstory reveal,
  a help-quest, a vulnerability beat (exactly Stardew's heart events).
- **Tracked NPCs:** Sorrel · Fen · Quill · Grindle · Wynn · Tamsin · Maren · Holloway · Otto.
  (Pike has no Rapport — he runs a separate wary/adversary track.)
- **Cost rule honored:** Rapport can stall or dip if neglected (you can't bank a friend and
  ignore them); deeper tiers add their own upkeep (a Confidant brings you their problems).

*STATSPINE addendum carries the per-arc Rapport spine for the principals (esp. Sorrel).*

## 4 — THE ROMANCE: Dr. Sorrel, light sapphic slow-burn

**Why her (commercial + structural):** the breakout cozy-fantasy comp (*Legends & Lattes*)
is sapphic slow-burn; Sorrel is a peer with natural proximity (the Mending Room), shared
stakes (she knew Edda), and shared mission — so the romance **braids with the grief-mystery**
instead of competing with it. Light tempo: a few beats, one quiet almost-moment, **no
kiss/declaration in Book 1** (the *Legends & Lattes* pace).

**The slow-burn beats (light; ride on Sorrel's Rapport track):**
- **Ep16–17 (Mending Room):** first real connection — two professionals, one quiet wry
  spark; Rapport → Friend. (Also her load-bearing Edda-thread entry.)
- **Ep28 (The Lull):** a domestic almost-moment in the warm dark — neither says it.
- **Ep31/34 (Edda/Rhea):** shared grief is the deepening — Rapport → Confidant; the
  romance and the mystery are the *same* tenderness.
- **Ep46 (meeting Edda):** Sorrel is the one who steadies her; the closest they come.
- **Ep48/49 (finale):** a soft, dry acknowledgment that this is *becoming* something —
  understated, hopeful, unresolved. Rapport → Dear; the Book-2 door, kept warm.

Voice guard: render it in the dry register — no swoon; the romance is in the *spaces*
(a held look, a second cup poured without asking), not declarations.
