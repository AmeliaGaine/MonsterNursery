## THE ONE THING THAT MOST RAISES MY SCORE

**The seasonal throughline as load-bearing structure, not decoration.** Most cozy
books treat weather as garnish sprinkled on after the plot. This outline makes
late-winter→high-summer the *spine* — the condemned frozen ruin literally thaws
into life as care takes hold, and season is wired as a "free, always-on clock that
deepens the read without manufacturing conflict." That's not a mood board; that's
an aesthetic THESIS. The snowdrop up through the frost (Ep10), the brook running
*because the nursery is alive again* (Ep16), the firefly dusk of the Lull (Ep28),
the storm-break sunrise (Ep49) — each lands on the emotional beat it belongs to.
And the gargoyle growing "a soft green coat as he's loved" is the single most
*me* detail in the whole document: the building's aliveness rendered as moss. That
is the kind of wonder I underline.

## THE ONE THING THAT MOST LOWERS MY SCORE

**The atmosphere is LABELLED with a confidence that an outline can't actually pay
out — and the document keeps *telling me it's cozy* instead of being cozy.** The
🌦️ weather-lines are lovely as a palette, but they read as *captions* — "First
fireflies at the warm dusk; the whole cast piled in wool and feather, three snores
in three keys." Gorgeous image! But it's a *label of a scene*, not the scene. The
real ASMR of process — the wipe-down cloth, the silver spoon that must never
whistle, the slow steep — is **named** ("a long quiet round of the evening
tending") far more often than it is *staged*. And the surrounding machinery is
loud: "F2-b · dedicated low-plot," "anti-plateau holds," "tachometer," "Seam
Report flag 4-residual." I understand it's an engineering doc, but for *my* axis
the danger is visible — this could become a spreadsheet in a quilt, where wonder is
asserted ("the System's deadpan drifts a half-degree warmer") on a schedule rather
than felt. The voice-guard line ("the fire had opinions about the draught," not
"rain wept silver") is exactly right and reassures me — but it's a *promise about
prose I can't see*. The outline earns the architecture of atmosphere. Whether it
earns the *texture* of it lives entirely in the unwritten sentences, and right now
the document is showing me the recipe card, not letting me taste the oats.
