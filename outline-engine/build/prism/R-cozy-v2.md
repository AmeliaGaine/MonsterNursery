# PRISM R — "Mara, the Cozy Found-Family Reader" — RE-RATING (v2)
### *The Monster Nursery, Book 1* — outline re-read after the cast expansion (RELATIONSHIPS recast)

*I read like I read Legends & Lattes and a House in the Cerulean Sea: I'm here for the warm place
that holds, the family that's more than a roster, the bittersweet send-off that earns a happy cry.
Last pass I gave this a 9 and left exactly one complaint on the table — the human family was thin.
I asked for one thing: give Fen a bond-line of her own and I'd round up. So I came back to read the
recast specifically to see whether the people around Liora got to be people. They did.*

### Did the human-cast gripe get fixed? **YES.**

Last time: *"Fen, Holloway, Otto barely register... give Fen a bond-line of her own and I'd round up to a 10."*

The recast paid that off and then some:
- **Fen** isn't set-dressing anymore — she gets a tracked arc (apprentice→keeper, seeded back at the feeding-hour where Cress wouldn't take from her hand) that *pays on the page* at Ep48: Liora crosses *apprentice* off the roster, writes *keeper*, and hands her the founding creature's round. "You've been doing the job. The board should say so." Rapport→Confidant, dry and plain. That's the bond-line I asked for, rendered exactly in the register I trust.
- **The three patrons** solve the deeper problem — that a give-away pipeline could feel like people passing through. Wynn/Tamsin/Maren *recur*: they come back, they bring the creatures back, they write letters. Sent-off creatures stay emotionally alive. The family reaches outward.
- **Grindle and Quill** turning from antagonists into allies-at-a-cost (Grindle lifting the pen and *setting it down*, initialling the hold knowing Pike will see whose hand it was) is the cozy "grump who softens" done with stakes. That's warmth I didn't have last read.

The one antagonist (Pike, off-page, a filed form) is the right call too — the cruelty stays abstract, which is what keeps me safe.

### The ONE thing still holding me back (if any)

**Honestly? Almost nothing — but if I'm being a careful reader: the het romance (Crane) is the one
new thread I'm rating on *faith* rather than on the page.** I love what it's *for* — the rivals-to-lovers
banter engine, the creatures voting him into the family against his will (the Corehound's three-way
vote, the Slime dissolving his Control-issue boots), the believer who can't file the lie and defects
at the seizure. On the outline it's structurally perfect and it's mercifully capped at "almost" (no
kiss, no declaration, the L&L pace I want). My only flicker of worry is that the *swoon* of a
rivals-to-lovers slow-burn lives or dies entirely in prose voice — and the outline is wisely keeping
it dry. If the drafting nails the held-look register without going stiff, it's a clean 10 with no
asterisk. It's a prose risk, not an outline flaw — so I'm not docking for it. I rounded up, gladly.

*(For the record: the worry that cost the point last time — the thin human cast — is gone. This is a
round, earned 10.)*
