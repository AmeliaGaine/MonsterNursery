## THE SPREAD — who it wins, who it caveats

**Bullseye (R = 9):** the core audience *adores* it. "A nursery I'd move into… every
send-off is a clean little heartbreak that leaves the porch cleaner than it found it…
the dead woman behind the deadpan is the warmest twist I've read all year." Not one
cruel beat survived to her.

**The two hardest-to-please-for-cozy-LitRPG segments both rate 8.5 — because of the
red-team fixes:**
- **Story/Mystery (Devon):** "*the one thing the genre keeps fumbling — it makes 'why
  you?' a clue you can verify, not a compliment the universe pays the hero.*" Explicitly
  credits the Cull Ledger + supersession fixes as "the difference between a 6 and an 8.5."
- **Voice/Humor (Priya):** "*the best comic engineering I've seen on a cozy-LitRPG
  blueprint*" — the three running gags with escalation timers, built to survive the
  fosters leaving.

**The two honest caveats:**
- **Progression (P = 7, the floor):** the cozy dials cap the ceiling for a hardcore
  progression reader — "*a very well-engineered climb up a gentle hill… the stakes can
  rise but never cut.*" This is a **deliberate lane trade-off**: it's a *cozy* LitRPG, not
  a combat one. Tomas recommends it to the Beware-of-Chicken crowd, not the DCC crowd —
  which is correct positioning, but it's the structural ceiling for that segment.
- **Immersion (I = 8):** atmosphere is "*beautifully scheduled*" but the texture "*lives
  entirely in unwritten sentences*" — the recurring prose-execution caveat.

## CROSS-CUTTING (flagged by ≥2 personas)
- **The human cast is thinner than the creature cast** (R + S). Mara: "*Fen, Holloway,
  Otto barely register… give Fen a bond-line and I'd round up to 10.*" Devon: "**Pike has
  no face yet**" — the living antagonist gets none of the installment-love the dead one
  does. This is the single highest-leverage remaining improvement for breadth.
- **Prose-execution is the universal ceiling** (I + M, echoed by P): the outline promises
  the architecture of cozy, wonder, and humor; whether it lands the *texture* and the
  *laugh-out-loud* is unprovable until drafted. (As every prior loop concluded.)

## VERDICT
**8.2/10 outline — with a 7 floor and a 9 ceiling, no DNF across the audience spectrum.**
The book this outline promises is a **strong-to-excellent cozy LitRPG that delights its
core audience and satisfies even the skeptical mystery and voice readers**, held just
below "universally breakout" by (a) a deliberate cozy-vs-progression-spike trade-off and
(b) prose-execution risk on atmosphere/humor. The one cross-segment fix that would lift
the floor: **make Pike a person and give 1–2 humans a real bond-line.**
