## Premise / central paradox
A burned-out Monster *Control* clerk (Liora Vale), whose profession is culling
monsters, becomes custodian of a Monster *Nursery* whose Sanctuary System rewards
care, not killing — and must prove "dangerous" creatures are children who can be
raised. Care is not the absence of danger; **care is the structure that turns danger
into safety.** *(OL 2A; C1 "Care is structural.")*

**Why inexhaustible:** the antagonist is a *fear-economy* (Monster Control budgets,
culling contracts, fear-sells-better newspapers), not a person. A working nursery is
a standing rebuke, so the apparatus keeps pushing back — inspection, petition, audit,
Commissioner. It can't be punched, only out-cared and out-maneuvered. *(OL 2A)*
