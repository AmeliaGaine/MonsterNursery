# ARCH-D.md — Retention-Forward Architecture

**Thesis: ARCH-D — Retention-forward.** Spine = the **Read-Through Engine**. The
whole book is reverse-engineered from two appetites: the *single finish-compulsion*
that drags a KU reader from Ep 1 to Ep 49 without surfacing, and the *single next-book
appetite* that makes closing Book 1 feel like an unfinished breath. Every arc climax
is engineered to do double duty: it pays its own promise in full (Guardrail G6) **and**
re-loads a bigger one. I optimize the binge — KU read-through %, Rising Stars
momentum, the 2am "one more episode."

*Anti-cliché protocol applied throughout: for each load-bearing choice I generated
three options, discarded the obvious one, and took the least-expected option that still
maximizes read-through. Discards are noted inline as `[discarded: …]`.*

## PART 2A — CENTRAL PARADOX ENGINE

### The locked paradox
A Monster **Control** clerk whose trade is *culling* becomes custodian of a Monster
**Nursery** whose System rewards *care*. The engine line, from canon:
**care is not the absence of danger; care is the structure that turns danger into safety.**

### Why it cannot exhaust (the retention reframe)
The antagonist is a **fear-economy**, not a person — Control budgets, culling
contracts, fear-sells-better newspapers. This is the single most important fact for a
read-through spine, because **a person can be beaten in one book and then the series is
over.** A fear-economy can only be *out-cared and out-maneuvered*, one institution at a
time, forever. The paradox is therefore not a question to answer but a **pressure that
ratchets**: every proof Liora makes that care works is a standing rebuke that *forces
the apparatus to escalate the next instrument* — clerk → inspector → petition → audit →
Commissioner. The book never runs out of antagonist because **success is the thing that
summons the next threat.** That is the finish-compulsion's fuel supply.

### The retention restatement of the paradox (my spine's version)
I restate the central paradox as a **read-through paradox**, so the binge mechanic is
load-bearing rather than decorative:

> Every episode must make the reader feel the nursery is **safe enough to relax into**
> AND **fragile enough that stopping feels like leaving the door open.**

Cozy is the floor (G1); precarity is the engine. The reader stops scrolling the instant
they believe the home is permanently safe. So the home is *never* permanently safe in
Book 1 — but (Guardrail G6) it is *always safe for now, warmly, by chapter's end.* The
compulsion lives in the gap between "for now" and "for good."

`[discarded: a single villain (Pike) as the engine — caps the series at his defeat]`
`[discarded: the mystery of Edda as the prime engine — that's ARCH-A's spine; here the
mystery is a fuel line into the binge, not the binge itself]`

## PART 2B — PROGRESSION BIBLE (six-axis)

Six texture-axes so advancement never flatlines into magnitude-only. Each axis is tied
to a **retention function**: what specifically it makes the reader unable to put down.
Stat names are canon (`CANON.md` stat spine); the strict skill order is honored.

| # | Axis | What grows | Retention function (why it holds the reader) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Magnitude** | Caregiver Rank (Trainee→Custodian I/II/III→Acting Matron→Matron); Bond Trust %; Comfort | The classic number-up dopamine. Used sparingly — a floor, not the engine. |
| 2 | **Access** | Rooms/habitats unlocked (per RB); skill-tree gates (Gentle Handling→…→Archive) | New *space* = new content the reader wants to see used. Each room is a question the next chapters answer. |
| 3 | **Texture** | Hearthglow (care-currency) and *how* it's earned — clean-with-care, restored function, reduced distress | Reframes progress as **moral texture**, not power. The reader roots for the *method*, not just the score. |
| 4 | **Social/Renown** | Reputation ladder (Stranger→Wary→Neutral→Town Ally→Regional); patron network | Each rung changes how the *world* treats Liora — visible status shifts are sticky. |
| 5 | **Mastery/Synergy** | Cast chemistry deployed as capability (synergy pairs as combos; frenemy/anti-synergy as managed risk) | The reader learns the cast like a deck; watching combos fire is its own pull. |
| 6 | **Lore/Archive** | Bestiary/Nursery Log entries; the Edda/Rhea/REDACTED thread | The deepening-mystery clock — the slow-drip that makes "stop here" feel like abandoning a question. |

### Per-arc axis assignment (cadence ladder)
Each arc **foregrounds a different axis** so the *kind* of growth rotates — this is what
defeats the mid-series plateau that kills read-through. (Magnitude is always quietly
present per G3's continuous stat chain; the named axis is the one carrying the arc.)

- **Arc 1 (Ep 1–12) — foreground AXIS 3 (Texture) + AXIS 2 (Access).**
  Establish the care-loop *and* the anti-cull hospitality (Welcome Desk, Tea Room) early
  — the differentiator can't wait. Reader learns the new currency (Hearthglow) and that
  *how* you win is the point.
- **Arc 2 (Ep 13–24) — foreground AXIS 4 (Social/Renown) + AXIS 2 (Access).**
  The placement pipeline opens; Liora's name starts to *mean* something in town. First
  send-off. Renown climbs Stranger→Town Ally.
- **Arc 3 (Ep 25–36) — foreground AXIS 6 (Lore) + AXIS 5 (Synergy).**
  Base-building fuses with the mystery (Archive, East Wing). The genre's sag zone becomes
  the peak because the *axis changes* exactly where most books flatten. Cast-as-deck pays
  off as the family operates as one organism (the Lull).
- **Arc 4 (Ep 37–49) — foreground AXIS 1 (Magnitude) + AXIS 4 (Social).**
  Everything earned is *spent defending the home* — the rank-up to Matron lands here, and
  Renown goes Regional. No new rooms (RB): every prior space becomes emotional stakes.

### Cost rule (anti power-creep, G3)
Every gain adds upkeep or a new management problem, per canon and RB. Schedule:

| Gain | Attached new problem |
|---|---|
| Brew Pool (Ep 9) | mis-steep → house-wide bitter tea (and morale) |
| Gargoyle mechanic (Ep 8) | sulks → a corner of Briarwick *sags* (Habitat Stability drops) |
| Fledgling Wing (Ep 13) | three incompatible needs in one room; fosters cycle in/out |
| West Wing sporeglow (Ep 18) | spreads if unmanaged |
| Higher Renown (Arc 2–4) | more patrons = more scrutiny = the apparatus notices you |
| Matron rank (Arc 4) | you are now the *named* target of the fear-economy |

The cruelest cost is structural: **the better the nursery does, the harder the system
pushes.** Success is the upkeep. That keeps magnitude from ever feeling safe.

### The dual-function retention insight
The fosters are a **renewable loop generator**: each one opens a placement question
(L-PLACE) that *must* close with a send-off — resolve + ache + "who arrives next?" That
last clause is a chapter-level next-appetite **built into the cast structure itself.**
The permanent family is the *anchor* (you never lose them — Promise 2); the fosters are
the *turnover* that keeps fresh faces, fresh problems, fresh send-offs cycling. Anchor +
turnover = a binge that never goes stale and never breaks the cozy contract.

## PART 2E — THE READ-THROUGH ENGINE (fully developed — the spine)

This is my load-bearing layer. Three components: the **one finish-compulsion**, the
**one next-appetite**, and the **chapter-level compulsion mechanic**.

### (1) THE ONE FINISH-COMPULSION (drives Ep 1 → Ep 49)

> **"Will Briarwick be *recognized as real* — a legitimate nursery the fear-economy
> can no longer touch — before the apparatus shuts it down?"**

This is the single question the whole book is reverse-engineered from. Why this and not
the obvious ones:

`[discarded: "Will Liora save the creatures?" — too diffuse; resolves per-creature, no
through-line]`
`[discarded: "Will Liora defeat Pike?" — a person can be defeated, which ends the binge
early and pre-spends the series villain]`

The chosen compulsion works because it is **a status the world grants, not a fight Liora
wins** — *legitimacy*. The fear-economy's whole power is that the nursery is *unofficial*,
*deniable*, *condemnable*. So the spine is a slow climb up the Renown ladder
(Stranger→Regional) where each rung is contested by the apparatus's next instrument. The
reader cannot stop because **the home is never yet safe-for-good** — and they've been
made to love it (G1 floor) precisely so the precarity bites. The compulsion is renewable
*within* the book (each arc raises the stakes of recognition) and **does not resolve the
series** (Book 1 wins *local/Regional* legitimacy; the fear-economy itself survives).

**The four arc climaxes, each paying its own promise AND doubling as the next hook:**

- **ARC 1 CLIMAX (Ep ~12) — "The First Inspection."**
  *Pays:* Inspector Maud Quill (persuadable) is brought in to find the nursery
  non-compliant; Liora wins the inspection by *care + cleverness* (G1/Promise 4) — the
  home survives its first official test. Warm-out: License Compliance crosses to viable.
  *Doubles as hook:* Quill, persuaded, lets slip that she answers to someone who *wanted
  this to fail* — the apparatus has a face now (Grindle), and a shadow above him. **The
  win proves care works, which is exactly why the system escalates.** → loads Arc 2.

- **ARC 2 CLIMAX (Ep ~24) — "The First Send-Off and the Petition."**
  *Pays:* the first foster is matched and sent off well (L-PLACE/L-KETTLE pays its ache);
  the nursery is now a *recognized placement service* — Renown hits Town Ally. The town
  shows up for Briarwick. Warm-out: the send-off porch at dawn.
  *Doubles as hook:* legitimacy at the town level triggers the *next tier* of the
  apparatus — a **petition/audit** filed from above, and the name **Pike** first lands
  with weight (L-PIKE seed). The community that just embraced the nursery is exactly the
  leverage the system will try to turn. **Being loved locally is what makes you worth
  auditing.** → loads Arc 3.

- **ARC 3 CLIMAX (Ep ~36) — "The Archive and the East Wing."**
  *Pays:* base-building fuses with mystery — restoring the Archive (Ep 31) and opening
  the East Wing (Ep 30) *is* the lore payoff; Liora learns **why care was outlawed** and
  that **Edda preserved the System's true purpose** against the fear-economy (L-EDDA
  partial pay; L-SPORE/L-EAST resolve). The family operates as one organism (the Lull).
  This is the emotional + lore peak where most books sag. Warm-out: the whole family
  asleep together — the thesis made literal.
  *Doubles as hook:* the Archive reveals the fear-economy was *engineered* — and points
  at its **architect, Commissioner Pike**, who now moves personally. The mystery's answer
  is a bigger threat: it's not bad luck, it's *design*, and the designer is coming. **The
  reason care was outlawed is also the reason it's about to be outlawed again.** → loads
  Arc 4.

- **ARC 4 CLIMAX (Ep ~49) — "The Seizure and the Recognition."**
  *Pays:* the apparatus moves to seize/condemn Briarwick (conflict-heat ≤4: seizure-by-
  bureaucracy, no gore, no creature lost — Promise 1/2/3/4). Liora defends the home with
  *everything earned* — Gargoyle holds (L-STAB), Corehound's alarm fires (set up Ep 22),
  the town stands up (L-FEAR), Quill turns, Edda's preserved purpose is invoked as the
  System's *own* authority against Control. Care + cleverness win; Briarwick is
  **recognized — Regional legitimacy** (the finish-compulsion is answered). The safe
  place endures (Promise 3). Warm-out: home, intact, *official*.
  *Doubles as hook → THE NEXT-APPETITE (below):* recognition reaches the **Regional**
  level, which is exactly the level that puts Briarwick on **Commissioner Pike's** desk —
  the architect of the fear-economy, the Book-2 adversary, behind the *series-door*. The
  win is the summons. **Becoming undeniable is what makes you Pike's problem.**

### (2) THE ONE NEXT-APPETITE (drives Book 1 → Book 2)

> **"Now that Briarwick is undeniable, the *architect* of the fear-economy — Commissioner
> Pike — has to deal with it personally. And the Archive proved the System was *deliberately*
> turned against care: someone is still keeping that lie running."**

Two threads braided into one appetite, both seeded and *partially* paid in Book 1, so the
reader ends fed but leaning forward:

- **The villain door (`L-PIKE`):** Book 1 beats the *instruments* (clerk, inspector,
  petition, audit). Book 2 must face the *architect*. Pike is named, glimpsed, and shown
  to be the *cause* — but never confronted. The reader's appetite: *I've watched her
  out-care every tool; now I have to see her out-care the toolmaker.*
- **The mystery door (`L-EDDA`/`L-RHEA`):** the Archive gives a *partial* answer (care was
  outlawed by design; Edda resisted) but withholds the last layer — **who Edda truly was,
  whether Rhea is the same thread, and what Edda's *final act of care* was** (`[OPEN]`:
  recommend the East Wing finale occupant **is** Edda's last protected creature/charge,
  making the locked room the literal seed of Book 2). The reader's appetite: *the System
  has a buried purpose, and someone is still hiding it.*

**Why this is the single strongest next-appetite:** it satisfies the cozy contract
completely (home safe, family intact, every warm loop closed) while leaving the *one*
thing a cozy-LitRPG reader will pre-order for — **the promise that the safe place they
love is about to face the person who could un-make it, and that there's still a secret
worth knowing.** Resolution + a bigger door, exactly per G6, scaled from chapter to book.

### (3) THE CHAPTER-LEVEL COMPULSION MECHANIC (the binge unit)

Read-through is won or lost at the *chapter boundary*, not the arc. The mechanic, applied
to every one of the 49 episodes:

**Each chapter runs the "Closed Loop, Open Door" two-beat ending:**
1. **Close a loop** the chapter itself opened or advanced (G6: full satisfaction —
   number-up, mystery clue, relationship beat, landed joke, or competence-display per G4's
   Cookie Law; **adjacent chapters never repeat the same cookie type**).
2. **Crack a door** one size larger — a new question, a noticed detail, an arriving
   patron, a System editorial note — that costs the reader nothing to want but everything
   to leave.

**The alternation rule (G6, operationalized for binge):** ends rotate
**warm-out → soft-hook → warm-out → hard-hook**, never two cruel hooks in a row, never
three warm-outs in a row. Concretely:
- **Warm-out** (the cozy floor, G1): a synergy-pair beat from `SM` (Toad↔Phoenix calm,
  Kraken↔Cress corner) — ready-made cozy enders.
- **Soft-hook:** a System editorial note in deadpan customer-service voice ("Note: Thank
  you for choosing care." / "Current Towel: legally insufficient.") that *implies* the next
  problem.
- **Hard-hook:** the live clock advances — an inspection date, a spreading spore, a
  sagging wall, a foster who *must* be placed before X — never a cruel cliffhanger, always
  resolved-then-bigger.

**The anti-plateau guarantee (G5):** every chapter carries **at least one live clock OR a
deepening-mystery drip** — no run of 2+ chapters with neither. The two clocks always
available: (a) a **placement clock** (a foster must be matched), and (b) the **apparatus
clock** (the next instrument is coming). The mystery drip (L-EDDA) runs continuously
under both. So even the coziest "low" chapter has a current pulling forward.

**The compulsion math (why it binges):** at any moment the reader is holding **2–4 live
loops** (per RB/`LOOPMAP` density) at different scales — a chapter-loop (closing now), an
arc-loop (the inspection/send-off/Archive), and the book-loop (recognition). Closing the
small one *feels* like a stopping point but the medium and large ones are still open and
just got *bigger* — so the natural place to stop is always one episode further than where
the reader is. That is the engine: **the safe pause never arrives until Ep 49 — and even
then, the door to Book 2 is ajar.**
