# ARCH-E.md — Heart-forward Architecture

*Architect: ARCH-E. Thesis: **Cast as Dual Functions.** Spine = found-family accretion.
Each arrival is one heart-beat AND one capability unlock, sequenced so the emotional
math compounds while the mechanical math chains (G3). Chemistry from `SM` is structural
fuel, not garnish. Resonance is engineered to stay in the dry register (G2: `COMMERCIAL`).*

> **Anti-cliché protocol applied per choice.** For each major decision I generated three
> options, discarded option 1 (the cliché), and took the least-obvious option that still
> maximizes read-through. The discarded clichés are logged inline as `~~cliché~~` so the
> Merge agent can audit the reasoning, not just the result.

## THE SPINE IN ONE SENTENCE

**Liora doesn't build a nursery; the nursery assembles Liora** — every creature that
arrives hands her one more piece of a self she culled out of herself years ago, and the
order of arrivals is the order in which a closed person is pried back open, each unlock
priced so the new love is also a new liability.

This is the load-bearing through-line. Mystery, cadence, loops, and retention all hang
off it. Where another spine asks *"what happens next?"*, the heart spine asks
*"who arrives next, and what does Liora get back when they do?"* — and answers it as a
**ladder of repair**, not a pile of pets.

# PART 2A — THE CENTRAL PARADOX ENGINE (heart-tuned)

## The paradox, restated through the cast
Canon: *care is the structure that turns danger into safety; the antagonist is a
fear-economy, not a person.* The heart-spine sharpens it to a **per-creature** engine:

> **Every creature is dangerous in exactly the shape of the care it needs.**
> The cockatrice petrifies *because* it is frightened of being seen as a weapon. The
> shriekmoth screams at light *because* light has only ever meant a culler's lantern.
> Danger is not a property of the monster; it is the unmet need wearing teeth. So the
> dual-function design is not a gimmick — it is the *thesis stated mechanically*: the
> capability you unlock IS the danger you metabolized by caring.

**Why this is inexhaustible (heart variant):** the fear-economy can be out-cared, but
Liora's *own* fear-economy — the internalized cull-clerk who measures everything in
liability — cannot be punched either. Book 1's real antagonist-in-miniature is the part
of Liora that still flinches when something dangerous loves her. Each creature retires
one flinch. That's a finite ladder (9 rungs) with an infinite floor (she will always
have one more flinch, which is the Book-2 door).

> Anti-cliché: ~~the burned-out clerk "rediscovers her warmth" in a montage~~ (cliché:
> warmth as a switch). ~~Warmth returns via a single saving-the-day catharsis~~ (cliché:
> warmth as a climax reward). **Chosen:** warmth returns *incrementally and against her
> will*, debited from her competence — every time she softens she becomes worse at the
> only job she was ever good at (liability-accounting), and that cost is on the page.

## The two pans of the scale (the engine's moving part)
Liora runs a permanent internal ledger: **Liability ↔ Belonging.** Every arrival drops
weight on both pans at once. Early arcs, the Liability pan is heavier and she manages it
like a clerk. The compounding mechanic: each creature's *belonging* weight is partly paid
by an *earlier* creature (chemistry = the synergy edges in `SM`), so the family
literally subsidizes its own newest member. By Arc 4 the Belonging pan is so loaded that
the fear-economy's threat ("this is all a liability — shut it down") reads as an attack
on a balance the reader has watched accrue, line by line.

**This is the finish-compulsion's root** (see 2E): the reader is watching a scale tip.

# PART 2B — THE PROGRESSION BIBLE (six-axis, heart-weighted)

Canon stat spine is fixed (Bond Trust %, Comfort, Habitat Stability %, License
Compliance %, Hearthglow, Renown, Caregiver Rank, Bestiary). Skill tree order is frozen.
The six texture-axes below are how growth *feels* so it never flatlines into
magnitude-only. **Heart-weighting:** every axis is expressed as a relationship moving,
not a number moving — the number is the receipt, the bond is the purchase.

| # | Axis | What advances | Heart expression (the spine's claim on this axis) | Cost it always adds (G3) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Magnitude** | Hearthglow, Comfort, capacity | More creatures sheltered = more love AND more upkeep mouths | every +capacity = +1 management problem |
| 2 | **Access** | Rooms, skill unlocks, Tea-Room matching | A new room is a new *member's* place at the table | rooms are liabilities (RB rule) |
| 3 | **Sophistication** | Resonance Cooking, Diagnosis, Ward-Mending tiers | Liora learns to read a creature's *real* need, not its threat-rating | finer care = finer ways to fail it |
| 4 | **Economy** | Pebbles, sporeglow, mood-teas, send-off fees | The family's quirks become the family's livelihood | each resource spreads/spoils if neglected |
| 5 | **Social/Renown** | Stranger→Regional ladder | The town stops fearing the family = the family can be *seen* loving | visibility invites the inspection/audit |
| 6 | **Lore/Mastery** | Edda thread, Archive, Caregiver Rank | Liora inherits a *purpose*, not just a job; becomes Matron | the more she belongs, the more she has to lose |

## Per-arc axis foregrounding (so advancement varies — borrowed discipline, heart-led)
Each arc leads with a different axis **anchored to a cast-acquisition wave**, so the
"new texture" the reader feels each arc is literally "a new kind of person joined the
house."

- **Arc 1 (Eps 1–12) — lead axis: ACCESS (3 founding bonds).** Growth = doors opening.
  Cress, Cinder, Bertram-formalized. The house becomes inhabitable. Magnitude is low on
  purpose so each early bond reads as enormous.
- **Arc 2 (Eps 13–24) — lead axis: ECONOMY + SOPHISTICATION (the fosters).** Growth =
  the family learning to *run* itself and to read need. The Fledgling Wing wave (Phoenix,
  Slime, Kettle) plus Tea Kraken's brew-economy. First send-off proves love can be a
  through-door, not only a wall.
- **Arc 3 (Eps 25–36) — lead axis: SOCIAL/RENOWN + LORE (the last two core).** Growth =
  being *seen*. Cockatrice's prejudice-buster + Corehound completing the family. The Lull
  (predator/prey asleep together) is the thesis made architectural. Mystery fuses in.
- **Arc 4 (Eps 37–49) — lead axis: MASTERY/MAGNITUDE-AS-STAKES.** No new core arrivals
  (withholding makes the built family the stakes). Growth = Liora becoming Matron by
  *defending* the accrued belonging without violence (Promise 4).

> Anti-cliché: ~~each arc just adds bigger creatures~~ (magnitude-only flatline). **Chosen:**
> each arc changes the *kind* of growth by changing *who joins and what they ask of her.*

# PART 2C — CAST AS DUAL FUNCTIONS *(the spine — fully developed)*

**The acquisition sequence is the structure.** Below, the FROZEN 9-creature cast is
ordered for **maximum emotional compounding**: each entry gives the creature's
(a) emotional role / the flinch of Liora's it retires, (b) mechanical contribution +
the canon stat/room it unlocks, (c) the **chemistry beat it unlocks** (the specific `SM`
edge that goes live the moment this creature exists), and (d) its episode anchor.

**The compounding rule (read this first):** arrivals are sequenced so that each new
member's *belonging* is purchased by a creature already present. The synergy edges fire
in an order that makes the family feel like it's catching its own newest member. That is
the heart-forward innovation: **acquisitions are not parallel, they are a relay.**

### WAVE 1 — THE FOUNDING THREE (Arc 1): "a closed person, pried open three times"

#### 1. Lunar Mooncalf — **Cress** · **Ep 1 (canon)**
- **Emotional role / flinch retired:** *the flinch of touching anything at all.* Cress is
  fragile, calm, asks for nothing but moonlight. The first creature is deliberately the
  one with the **lowest liability weight** — Liora can care for it without admitting she's
  caring. It retires the flinch "if I touch it, I'll be responsible for it." (She already
  is; the System has noticed.)
- **Mechanical contribution:** opens the care-loop itself. **Unlocks:** Moonwell Room;
  Bond (Cress) 19%; Comfort 1; Gentle Handling I; first Hearthglow drip.
- **Chemistry beat unlocked:** none yet (Cress is the seed). But its *glow* arms two future
  edges (Kraken↔Cress synergy; Shriekmoth↔Cress anti-synergy) — the moonlight is a
  loaded gun the cast fires later. Spine note: Cress is the **subsidy source** for the
  whole found-family; nearly every later cozy corner borrows its calm.
- **Cookie (G4):** relationship beat + first number-up.

> Anti-cliché: ~~open with the most spectacular monster~~ (cliché: lead with the dragon).
> **Chosen:** open with the creature that asks for the *least*, so the reader feels Liora
> say yes to the smallest possible thing and still flinch. The smallness is the hook.

#### 2. Ember-Toad — **Cinder** · **Ep 4 (canon)**
- **Emotional role / flinch retired:** *the flinch of heat = harm.* A cull-clerk's reflex:
  anything that runs hot in a wooden building is a fire hazard to be removed. Cinder
  retires it via the line the room is built on — *"heat is not harm when given somewhere
  safe to go."* This is the first creature whose **danger is real** (fire-risk) and is
  metabolized rather than removed. It teaches Liora the thesis in her hands.
- **Mechanical contribution:** **Unlocks:** Basking Nook 30%→up; Comfort 2; fire-safety
  sub-system; Habitat Mending I; appetite/feeding economy seed.
- **Chemistry beat unlocked:** **Cinder is the keystone of the chemistry graph.** Its
  arrival pre-arms (i) the Toad↔Kraken **frenemy** dial (perfect steep vs scalding
  catastrophe — the arc-pressure lever per `SM`), (ii) Toad↔Phoenix **synergy** (the ember
  that calms the panic-bird — paid forward to Wave 2), and (iii) Toad↔Slime **anti-synergy**
  (fire vs water standoff). Cinder is *acquired early on purpose* so its chemistry can
  subsidize three later arrivals.
- **Cookie:** competence-display (she handles a fire-risk by care) + landed joke (appetite).

#### 3. Blink Gargoyle — **Bertram** · *present Ep 1; mechanic formalized Ep 8*
- **Emotional role / flinch retired:** *the flinch of "this place isn't mine / won't
  hold."* Bertram is load-bearing — literally the building's will to stand. He's already
  here, like grief furniture. Formalizing his mechanic (Ep 8) is the beat where Liora
  stops treating Briarwick as a posting and starts treating it as a *body she's
  responsible to.* He says "Mm." and the house agrees to stay up for her.
- **Mechanical contribution:** **Unlocks:** Habitat Stability % as a live mechanic;
  security/"fast-travel" via blink; building-spirit throughline. **New problem (G3):** he
  sulks, blinks off-post, and a corner **sags** — the base has moods.
- **Chemistry beat unlocked:** Gargoyle↔Slime **anti-synergy** (blinks into a fresh puddle:
  "Mm.") — *deferred-armed*, fires once Slime arrives Arc 2. Gargoyle↔Cockatrice
  **synergy** (two unbothered stones) armed for Arc 3. Bertram is the **second subsidy
  hub**: his stability props every future room.
- **Cookie:** mystery-deepened (a building with a will hints at Edda) + landed deadpan.

> Anti-cliché on Wave 1 ordering: ~~founding trio = the three cutest creatures~~ (cliché).
> **Chosen ordering logic:** *lowest-liability → first-real-danger → the place itself.*
> The reader watches Liora say yes to a feeling (Cress), then to a risk (Cinder), then to
> a *home* (Bertram). Three escalating sizes of commitment, all before the fosters arrive.

**Wave-1 compounding check:** by Ep 8 the house has calm (Cress), warmth + a chemistry
engine (Cinder), and a will to stand (Bertram). The next wave's bonds will be *paid for*
by these three. Belonging pan: light but loaded. Liability pan: still heavier — Liora is
still counting.

### WAVE 2 — THE FOSTERS (Arc 2): "love that's allowed to leave"

The fosters are the **engine of the book's rarest dopamine: the send-off** (RB). Heart
function: they teach Liora — and the reader — that *belonging is not the same as keeping.*
Each foster's danger is metabolized into a placeable virtue, then the creature *leaves
well.* This is where sentimentality is most dangerous and most controlled (G2): the ache
is real but the prose stays dry and the plot keeps moving (a match is a goal with a clock).

#### 4. Pocket Phoenix · **Ep 13** (Fledgling Wing opens)
- **Emotional role / flinch retired:** *the flinch of "fragility is failure."* The phoenix
  self-combusts to ash when startled — it dies of nerves, constantly, harmlessly. Liora
  the clerk would log it as unstable/unplaceable. Caring for it retires the flinch that a
  thing which keeps falling apart isn't worth the upkeep. *Its arc: stop dying of nerves.*
- **Mechanical contribution:** **Unlocks:** Boarding capacity +; Bond (Phoenix) seeded;
  Soothing Song input; the *placement pipeline* (`L-PLACE`) goes live.
- **Chemistry beat unlocked — THE COMPOUNDING PAYOFF:** **Toad↔Phoenix synergy fires.**
  The ember Liora acquired in Ep 4 is *why* the phoenix can be soothed in Ep 13 — Cinder
  is the calmest thing in the building, and the panic-bird stops combusting beside it. The
  reader feels an earlier acquisition pay an interest dividend. Also arms the **disaster
  triangle:** Phoenix↔Shriekmoth and Phoenix↔Cockatrice anti-synergies (flare→shriek→
  combust feedback loop), held for later comic mileage.
- **Cookie:** relationship beat + the first visible "old bond subsidizes new bond" moment.

> Anti-cliché: ~~the phoenix's arc is "learns to be brave"~~ (cliché). **Chosen:** the
> phoenix never stops being anxious — it finds the *one warm spot* (Cinder) where anxiety
> is survivable, and that's the placeable virtue a patron is matched to. Care = fit, not fix.

#### 5. Puddle Slime · **Ep 13–15** (same wing, staggered intake)
- **Emotional role / flinch retired:** *the flinch of "messy = worthless / dispose of it."*
  The slime leaves dissolving puddles (slip-hazard) that also *dissolve grime* (useful).
  Pure cull-clerk logic says: liability, remove. The reframe — *the mess is the gift* —
  retires Liora's tidiest, coldest reflex.
- **Mechanical contribution:** **Unlocks:** cleaning-economy resource (clean-with-care →
  Hearthglow); Habitat Mending II input; the running slip-gag.
- **Chemistry beat unlocked:** **Slime↔Gargoyle anti-synergy finally fires** (Bertram's
  Ep-8 deferred gag pays off — he blinks into a fresh puddle, "Mm."). Also arms
  **Cockatrice↔Slime synergy** (pebble-paths corral the puddles — *two problems solving
  each other*), held for Arc 3 as a structural rhyme: the family fixes itself.
- **Cookie:** landed joke (the gargoyle splash) + economy number-up.

#### 6. Shriekmoth — **Kettle** · **Ep ~15–17** (the hub of friction; placed last of wave)
- **Emotional role / flinch retired:** *the flinch of "some creatures are just
  incompatible — cut your losses."* Kettle screams at any light; it clashes with
  *everything luminous* (`SM` names it the friction hub). It is the hardest to love and
  the hardest to place. Loving it retires Liora's deepest cull-clerk axiom: that some
  things don't fit and fitting can't be built. *Its arc: a happy shriek that chimes, not
  screams.*
- **Mechanical contribution:** **Unlocks:** Ward-Mending I (the velvet-dark alcove is a
  ward); completes the Fledgling Wing's three-microclimate puzzle; Corehound↔Shriekmoth
  security-system seed (paid off Arc 3/4).
- **Chemistry beat unlocked:** the **full anti-synergy storm** goes live — Shriekmoth↔Cress
  (glow→shriek ruins the coziest room), Shriekmoth↔Phoenix (the feedback-loop disaster),
  Corehound↔Shriekmoth synergy (alarm+shriek = elite security) pre-armed. Kettle is the
  cast's **chaos multiplier** — placing it is the hardest matching problem, which makes
  its **send-off the book's biggest relief-and-heartbreak** (`SM`).
- **Cookie:** competence-display (Liora solves the unsolvable fit) + mystery (why does
  light terrify it? — culler's lanterns; a quiet rhyme with Liora's old job).

**THE FIRST SEND-OFF · Ep 20 (Send-Off Porch opens).** Heart-spine peak of Arc 2: a
fostered creature leaves with the *right* patron. Resolve + ache + new appetite. **Which
foster leaves first is a deliberate choice:** ~~Kettle (clear the friction)~~ — no, too
relieving, the reader should *want* it to stay. ~~Phoenix~~ — too easy. **Chosen:** the
**Slime** goes first — useful, low-drama, genuinely beloved-by-now — so the first send-off
teaches the reader that *even the easy ones hurt to give away.* That sets the price of
Kettle's eventual send-off (later/Book-2-adjacent) sky-high.

> The send-off is the heart-spine's masterstroke against sentimentality: love is proven
> *commercial* (a transaction with a clock and a patron) and *true* (it costs) in the same
> beat. G2 satisfied by construction.

**Wave-2 compounding check:** the founding three now *subsidize* the fosters (Cinder calms
Phoenix; Bertram/Cress chemistry frames the wing; the cleaning-economy monetizes Slime).
The family has learned to fund its newcomers. Belonging pan rising fast.

### WAVE 3 — THE LAST TWO CORE (Arc 3): "the family completes, and is seen"

These two finish the permanent found-family. Their dual function shifts the lead axis to
**Social/Renown + Lore** — the family stops being private and becomes *visible*, which is
both the warmest beat (the town comes around) and the door to the Arc-4 threat (visibility
invites audit).

#### 7. Tea Kraken · **Ep 9 (Brew Pool) — present from Arc 1, bond-completed Arc 2/3**
*(Canon places the Brew Pool at Ep 9; the Kraken is acquired early but its **heart-role
matures late**, which is the point — see below.)*
- **Emotional role / flinch retired:** *the flinch of "I don't get to be comfortable."*
  The Kraken steeps the house tea, surfaces a tentacle to steal a biscuit — it makes the
  building *hospitable to Liora herself.* Its slow-burn role: it's the creature that cares
  for the *carer.* Retires the flinch that comfort is something she dispenses, never
  receives.
- **Mechanical contribution:** **Unlocks:** Brew Pool; mood-teas (buff meals); Resonance
  Cooking II input; feeds the Tea Room hospitality engine (Ep 10) → the matching mechanic
  that the *whole foster economy* runs on. Mechanically, the Kraken is the **economic
  backbone** of Arc 2's placements.
- **Chemistry beat unlocked:** **Kraken↔Cress synergy** (warm tea + cool moonlight = the
  single coziest corner; the ASMR set-piece) — the book's signature warm-out, *built from
  the two earliest acquisitions.* And the **Toad↔Kraken frenemy dial** — the escalation
  lever the writer pushes for gentle rising pressure (perfect steep vs scalding house-wide
  tea catastrophe). The Kraken is where chemistry becomes *plot pressure.*
- **Cookie:** competence + the coziest landed warm-out in the book.

> Acquisition-timing note (anti-cliché): ~~introduce the Kraken with a splashy reveal~~
> (cliché). **Chosen:** the Kraken is *quietly already steeping tea* (Brew Pool Ep 9) but
> its heart-function — caring for Liora — is only *named* once she's soft enough to notice
> she's being cared for (Arc 2/3). The creature was loving her before she let herself feel
> it. That delayed recognition is a pure read-through dividend.

#### 8. Cockatrice Chick · **Ep 26 (Pebble Garden)**
- **Emotional role / flinch retired:** *the flinch of "what the town calls deadly must be
  deadly."* The cockatrice petrifies — and *fails*, leaving only warm pebbles. It looks
  like a weapon; it is a frightened child making collectible rocks. Loving it publicly
  retires Liora's last inherited reflex: deferring to the fear-economy's threat-rating
  over her own eyes. *This is the prejudice-buster, and it's the most political love in
  the book* — it's the one the town can *see* and must be argued with.
- **Mechanical contribution:** **Unlocks:** Pebble Garden; **pebbles as economy resource**
  (paths, trim, gifts); Renown/`L-FEAR` thread goes fully live; Public Handling skill;
  Advocacy seed. Bestiary +1.
- **Chemistry beat unlocked:** **Cockatrice↔Gargoyle synergy** (two unbothered stones; the
  death-glare does nothing, the gargoyle respects the effort) — Bertram's Ep-8 deferred
  synergy pays off. **Cockatrice↔Slime synergy** (pebble-paths corral the puddles) — the
  family literally solving its own earlier problem on-page. Plus Cockatrice↔Phoenix
  anti-synergy (the scary look still spooks the bird). The cockatrice **closes** more
  chemistry loops than any other creature — it's the relay's final hand-off.
- **Cookie:** number-up (Renown) + competence (Advocacy) + the deflation-gag engine.

> Anti-cliché: ~~the town is won over by a single heroic rescue~~ (cliché). **Chosen:** the
> town is won over by *pebbles* — the creature's failures become the town's footpaths and
> windowsill gifts; people are caring for the cockatrice's habitat-trim *before they
> realize they've forgiven it.* Renown earned by infrastructure, not heroism.

#### 9. Corehound · **Ep 22 (Three-Temper Kennel) — completes the family**
*(Canon places the Kennel at Ep 22; structurally the Corehound is the **family-completing
keystone** — placed near the Arc-2/3 hinge so the core five close just as the fosters
start leaving.)*
- **Emotional role / flinch retired:** *the flinch of "you can't satisfy everyone, so don't
  try."* Three heads, three thermostats, zero agreement — a tiny internal civil war. It is
  the **found-family thesis compressed into one body**: irreconcilable needs, all worth
  meeting. Loving it retires Liora's last *managerial* flinch — that care must triage. The
  Corehound proves you don't triage a family; you build three beds.
- **Mechanical contribution:** **Unlocks:** Three-Temper Kennel; **early-warning/alarm
  system** (alarm-head) — the security mechanic that pays off in the Arc-4 inspection;
  Bond (Corehound) seeded; Habitat Stability +1. **New problem (G3):** three thermostats
  that refuse to compromise.
- **Chemistry beat unlocked:** **Corehound↔Shriekmoth synergy** (alarm-head + light-shriek
  = elite, deeply unpleasant security — pays off Kettle's Ep-15 seed in the Arc-4 defense).
  **Kraken↔Corehound anti-synergy** (tentacle steals the biscuit; the food-head is
  *outraged*, the other two don't care). **Corehound↔itself** (the running civil war = a
  cookie on demand). The Corehound is the cast's **renewable comedy reactor.**
- **Cookie:** landed joke (three-way argument) + competence (alarm system) — and the
  family-complete emotional beat.

> Anti-cliché: ~~the family is completed by a big loyal guardian~~ (cliché: the Good Boy).
> **Chosen:** the family is completed by the *least unified creature in the building* — a
> three-way argument with fur — so "family complete" doesn't mean "harmony achieved," it
> means "we hold three incompatible things at once and call it home." That's the thesis,
> not a hug.

**THE LULL · Ep 28 (Arc-3 peak).** Predator and prey piled together asleep — *all three
Corehound heads finally agree on one thing: nap.* The found-family made architectural.
This is the **emotional summit of the acquisition spine**: every creature acquired across
49 episodes is in one warm dark room, and the only thing that could threaten it is the
fear-economy at the door (Arc 4). Belonging pan now heaviest.

> G2 guard: the Lull is rendered in the dry register — inventory, breathing, three snores
> in three keys — not a lyrical aria. The warmth is in the *list*, not the adjectives.

# PART 2D — THE PROMISE LEDGER (seed → payoff, heart-threaded)

# PART 2E — THE READ-THROUGH ENGINE

## The ONE finish-compulsion (why you can't put Book 1 down)
> **"The scale is tipping and I have to watch it land."**
> From Ep 1, Liora runs the Liability↔Belonging ledger, and every creature drops weight on
> both pans. The reader internalizes the scale as the book's true progress bar. By Arc 4
> the Belonging pan is visibly, *accruedly* heavy — the fear-economy's threat is an attack
> on a balance the reader has personally watched build, creature by creature, dividend by
> dividend. **You finish because you've been keeping the ledger too, and you need to see
> the Belonging pan win without anyone you love getting hurt** (Promise 2). The
> compounding cast-relay is what makes the final weight *feel earned rather than asserted* —
> that's the whole engine. The mechanical receipt: Hearthglow and Bond totals are highest
> at the moment of maximum threat, so the numbers and the heart peak together.

> Anti-cliché: ~~the finish-compulsion is "will the villain be defeated?"~~ (cliché, and it
> violates Promise 4: no violence). **Chosen:** the compulsion is *will the warmth hold its
> ground?* — a defense, not a victory; you read on to watch a home *endure* (Promise 3),
> which is a cozier and rarer pull than triumph.

## The ONE next-appetite (why you must start Book 2)
> **"There is one more creature waiting to be sent off — and one more flinch in Liora that
> no creature has retired yet."**
> Two interlocked hooks, both heart, both seeded not dangled: **(1)** Kettle's send-off is
> deliberately withheld (the hardest love to give away is the one Book 2 opens on); and
> **(2)** the East Wing / Edda thread reveals that Liora has been finishing an *interrupted*
> send-off from a generation ago — meaning the work is generational, the fear-economy has a
> *person* now (Commissioner Pike, canon Book-2 adversary), and Liora's last flinch
> ("can I protect a family this big when the apparatus comes with a name?") is the Book-2
> spine. You start Book 2 because the found-family is complete *and therefore newly
> vulnerable* — the bigger the belonging, the bigger the door that opens.

## The retention machine, per chapter (G4/G5 honored by the spine)
- **Every chapter pays a cookie** because the cast-relay guarantees one: any anti-synergy
  edge = a landed joke on demand; any synergy edge = a warm-out; any arrival = number-up +
  relationship; any chemistry-dividend = competence-display. The chemistry map (`SM`) is a
  literal cookie-vending machine the writer draws from.
- **Adjacent chapters vary the cookie** by alternating cast registers: an anti-synergy
  comedy chapter is followed by a synergy warm chapter or a mystery/lore chapter (never two
  jokes from the same edge in a row).
- **A clock or mystery is always live** (G5): in Arc 1 the founding-bond clocks (will Cress
  trust? will the fire-risk hold?); Arc 2 the placement clocks (`L-PLACE` matches have
  patron-arrival deadlines); Arc 3 the Renown clock + Edda mystery; Arc 4 the inspection
  clock. The Liability↔Belonging scale is the meta-clock that never pauses.
- **Warm-out / hook-out alternation** (G6): send-offs and synergy beats are the warm-outs;
  mystery reveals and inspection escalations are the hook-outs; the spine schedules them so
  no two cruel hooks ever sit adjacent and no three warm-outs run flat.

# GUARDRAIL SELF-AUDIT (ARCH-E)

- **G1 LANE — PASS.** Every threat targets home / found-family / way-of-life (inspection,
  audit, town-fear, fire-risk, the interrupted send-off). No existential grimdark; no
  toothless conflict (real fire-risk, real prejudice, real seizure-by-bureaucracy).
  Conflict-heat ≤ 4. Darkness aimed outward at the fear-economy, metabolized into warmth.
- **G2 NOT-LITERARY — PASS · token `COMMERCIAL`.** The heart is delivered as *plot*: bonds
  are goals with clocks, send-offs are transactions with patrons, the Liability↔Belonging
  ledger is a legible want every scene. Resonance lives in the dry register (lists,
  inventory, deadpan), never lyrical opacity. Payoffs are delivered (the dividend table),
  not withheld as theme. The two C1 lyrical lines are NOT reproduced.
- **G3 STAT CONTRACT — PASS.** The acquisition relay *is* the unlock chain: nothing fires
  before its seed (Toad↔Phoenix dividend requires Cinder@Ep4 before Phoenix@Ep13;
  Cockatrice synergies require Bertram@Ep8 + Slime@Ep13). Every arrival adds upkeep / a new
  problem (sag, fire-risk, slip-hazard, spread, three thermostats). No free power.
- **G4 COOKIE LAW — PASS.** Chemistry map guarantees ≥1 cookie/chapter; adjacency rule
  varies the type.
- **G5 ANTI-PLATEAU — PASS.** A live clock or mystery in every arc (founding-bond → placement
  → Renown+Edda → inspection), under the never-pausing scale meta-clock.
- **G6 NO CLIFFHANGER CRUELTY — PASS.** Every arc resolves its own promise then opens a
  bigger door (Arc1: home inhabitable → who do we foster?; Arc2: first send-off lands →
  can the unplaceable be placed?; Arc3: family complete + seen → the apparatus arrives;
  Arc4: home defended without violence → generational work / Book-2 Pike). Warm-out/hook-out
  alternation enforced. The two Book-2 carries are appetite, not dangle.
