## PART 3 — HEARTHGLOW AS A REAL ECONOMY (the cost that bites)

*Per the analysis-swarm critique: costs must BITE. Hearthglow is earned ONLY by care
(clean-with-care, restored function, reduced distress, warmth freely given) and
**decays/drains** under upkeep — it is a leash, not an accelerator (ARCHITECTURE
§2B.6). It is a finite reserve, not a meter that only rises.*

### The forced trade-off — ARC 2, ~Ep 17–18 (the genuine "can't afford to save this right now")

**Setup (all Ledger-legal at this point):**
- Ward-Mending I unlocks Ep17 and immediately puts wards on **standing Hearthglow
  upkeep** (S7 cost).
- The Mending Room (Ep16) must be stocked; the West Wing sporeglow (Ep18) starts
  **spreading and must be contained** (room cost).
- Hearthglow entered Arc 2 at **9** and is draining toward **7**.

**The bite:** three care-needs land in the same window, and the reserve covers only
two:

1. **Ward the Fledgling Wing** so the staggered fosters (Phoenix nerves, Kettle's
   light-terror) settle — keeps `L-PHX`/`L-KETTLE` on track.
2. **Contain the spreading sporeglow** before it reaches the larder stores — a
   habitat/safety need with a hard clock.
3. **Splurge a full Resonance Cooking II buff-steep** to fast-track a *fragile
   creature's* comfort (the emotionally "obvious save").

Liora cannot fund all three without dropping Hearthglow below the ward-upkeep floor —
which would **collapse the wards she just raised**, undoing the foster progress. She
must **choose to defer the buff-steep** — let a creature stay uncomfortable *for now* —
because spending on the heart-choice would starve the structural ones. The System notes
it in deadpan ("Reserve insufficient for elective comfort. Triage logged."). The reader
feels the floor: **this is the episode where you cannot afford to save everything at
once.** It is recovered deliberately in Arc 3 (Hearthglow climbs 7→12) so Arc 4 has
something to *spend*.

### The second bite — ARC 4 climax (the reserve spent to the floor)

During the seizure (Eps 44–45), holding every creature calm + powering wards + funding
the petition **drains Hearthglow from 12 to ~3**. There is no surplus; the home is
defended on the last of the reserve. The win is *earned at the bottom of the jar*, not
from abundance — which is why it reads as survival, not a power-fantasy. By Ep49 it has
ticked back only to a quiet **3** (warm, but visibly spent — Book-2 fuel).

> **Why this satisfies the critique:** Hearthglow can go **down** and can hit a wall
> where a save is unaffordable *right now*; the trade-off is a real choice with a real
> casualty (deferred comfort), not a speed bump; and the currency is structurally
> coupled to upkeep so it can never become a free accelerator. **Costs bite.**
