## Scene 1 — The door behind the flour   [S1. Open in motion]
- **Function / POV state:** Drop mid-action on a concrete running WANT — no scene-setting. The seam from Ep17 carries: Ward-Mending I went online yesterday and put a *standing* Hearthglow drain on the house; Liora opened the morning already counting, because the reserve ticked 9→8 overnight and she has a list. WANT: get into the West Wing larder, which she's never been able to open, because the Fledgling Wing wards are eating reserve and she needs the larder's *stores* to stretch the feeding budget. A supply errand. That is all it is, this morning.
- **Beats:**
  - Open on Liora already shouldering the swollen West Wing door — the one that's been painted shut since before her key — because the wards she raised yesterday (Ep17) cost reserve every hour they hold, and a larder full of dry stores is the cheapest way to feed the house without spending Hearthglow she doesn't have. Practical. A clerk's errand.
  - The door gives. Cold comes out first — damp, cellar-old, sweetish-rotten under it. Then the *light*: a faint cold green laid over the shelves like frost that glows, running the mortar-lines, furring the old crocks. Living lichen. It is beautiful and it is *eating her stores*, and the second thing registers a beat before the first finishes being the first.
  - Diagnosis (I @7, II @16) flags it without being asked, and flags two things at once: the lichen is a *resource* (feed-grade, heal-grade — it reads as nourishing) **and** it is *spreading*, a soft front of it advancing on the dry-store shelves at the back where the winter grain is. A larder that is also an organism, and the organism is hungry the same direction she is.
- **System box:** `> Room unlocked: West Wing larder. Designation: living. Contents: sugar-spore lichen (feed-grade / heal-grade) AND your winter stores, currently being reclassified as feed-grade by the lichen. Note: this is a pantry. It is also a tenant. It has not signed anything.`
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** Damp larder dark, the sporeglow a faint cold green furring the mortar; cellar-cold and a sweetish under-smell of slow sugar; through the one cracked sill behind her, spring — blossom and the drone of drowsy bees that, she'll notice in a moment, keep drifting *in* toward the green and not out.
- **Lands here:** **West Wing larder unlocks** (rooms-bible @18; sporeglow resource live; "spreads if unmanaged" is the new standing problem). `L-SPORE` foreground clock **arms** — the front is advancing on the stores with a hard edge. The supply errand has become a containment job. Comfort holds; nothing harmed.
- **Exit:** She came down for grain and found a creature wearing the larder. She will have to contain it before it reaches the stores — which means spending. She goes up to count what she has to spend, already knowing it isn't enough.

## Scene 2 — Two funds, three needs   [S2. Escalating want vs. obstacle]
- **Function / POV state:** The engine beat — give it the most room. WANT: cover the morning's care-needs out of the reserve. The obstacle is *arithmetic she cannot argue with* — three live needs, a reserve that funds two — and every time she tries to make it three, the math answers back harder. This is the Hearthglow bite, built as a real triage.
- **Beats:**
  - **The ledger laid out (the three needs, all Ledger-legal, all genuinely owed):** Liora sits with the reserve at **8** and Ward-Mending's standing drain already booked against it, and counts what care actually costs this hour. **(1) Hold the Fledgling Wing wards** — raised yesterday for the staggered fosters; let them lapse and Kettle's light-terror and the Phoenix's nerves come straight back, undoing the foster progress she just banked. Non-optional; it's the floor. **(2) Contain the sporeglow** before its front reaches the winter stores — a habitat-and-safety need with a hard clock (Scene 1's advancing edge); let it go and the larder eats the food that feeds everyone. Non-optional; it's a different floor. **(3) Splurge a full Resonance Cooking II buff-steep** — RC II came online *today* — to fast-track the comfort of the one creature visibly suffering for it: the **Pocket Phoenix**, anxious to the point of guttering, who would, with a proper buff-steep, settle days early. The obvious save. The *heart* save. The one any carer would reach for first.
  - **Try 1 — make it three (cut elsewhere):** She runs it like a clerk and tries to find slack. There isn't any. The wards' drain is fixed (Ep17 cost); the containment can't be done by hand alone — sporeglow has to be *fed back into itself* with warmth and reserve or it advances. Both structural needs are hard floors, and together they sit a hair under the reserve. The buff-steep is the one elastic line, and it's the one with a *frightened animal* attached to it.
  - **Try 2 — do it cheap (half-measures):** She tries to half-fund all three. She learns the cruelty of the floor: a half-warded wing is an *un*-warded wing (the wards either hold or they don't); a half-contained lichen *still spreads*, just slower toward the same stores; and a half-steep is a mis-steep, which — RC II's own cost — *backfires* into morale, and on a creature already this fragile would do harm, not good. Half of three is not one-and-a-half saves. It's three failures wearing thrift. **[IDLE-TICK]** the System posts the running drain mid-count, unbothered.
  - **Try 3 — costs the most (look at the Phoenix and do the math anyway):** She goes up to the Fledgling Wing and *looks* at the thing the arithmetic is asking her to defer. The Phoenix is in the corner it doesn't like, feathers ticking with the small constant tremor Diagnosis II named as *chronic, not acute* — a nerves-condition that wants ongoing care, not one fix. She could steep it calm by nightfall. She has the skill as of this morning. She does not have the reserve — not without dropping below the ward-floor and collapsing the wing she just stabilized. The most expensive try is the one where she stands in front of the creature she could comfort and *chooses* the two cold structural saves over the warm obvious one, because comforting this one would starve the two that keep all of them safe.
- **System box:** **[IDLE-TICK]** mid-count — `> Hearthglow reserve: 8, draining (ward upkeep, active). Pending requests: ward-hold (structural), spore-containment (structural), comfort-steep (elective). Note: reserve funds two of three. The System does not choose for you. The System only notes that it noticed you choosing.`
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** The ledger-light of a spring noon laid flat on the reserve-tally; up in the Fledgling Wing, the Phoenix's tremor and the small dry sound of a creature trying not to combust from nerves; the wards humming their faint warm cost; everywhere the smell of blossom through the sash, which is no help at all.
- **Lands here:** the **triage is fully built and felt** — three owed needs, a two-fund reserve, no honest way to make three. `L-SPORE` clock stays foreground and tight (containment is one of the two she *must* fund). No stat moves yet — the cost is the *decision*, paid in the next beat. Cozy floor holds: nobody is harmed; the harm she's weighing is *deferred comfort*, not danger.
- **Exit:** She has the arithmetic and hates it. There is no skill on the tree for *affording everything.* She goes back down to the larder to do the two saves the reserve will fund — and to leave the third undone, on purpose, with the creature watching.

## Scene 3 — Contain it, ward it, and leave the steep cold   [S3. The yes-but turn · COOKIE LANDS HERE]
- **Function / POV state:** THE TURN. The cookie and the complication arrive on the same beat (yes-but). WANT met: she funds the two structural saves and they *work* — the wing holds, the lichen is contained. But the act of containing it recontextualizes the whole room, and the recontextualization is the mystery — while the third need stays pointedly, payingly undone.
- **Beats:**
  - Liora spends the reserve where the arithmetic told her: holds the Fledgling Wing wards (the floor stays under the fosters) and contains the sporeglow — not by killing it, but by *feeding it back on itself*, warming a boundary-line with reserve and a cloth off Cinder's heat so the lichen grows *along* the wall instead of *across* the stores. Care-as-containment: she doesn't cull the organism, she gives it somewhere safe to grow. It works. The advancing front stops at the warmed line and turns, tracing the mortar the way warmed sporeglow wants to.
  - **Yes-but (the cookie lands on the same beat):** the lichen, fed and turned along the boundary, grows *toward the warmth she laid down* — and as it climbs the back wall it lights, cold and green and even, the exact rectangle of something **painted over.** A seam. A door-line, or a panel-line, sealed and over-painted and hidden, that the sporeglow now outlines in glowing relief because the paint runs cold and the wall behind it runs *warm* — there is a space back there. The lichen didn't reveal it by accident; it grew toward warmth, and something behind that seam is *warm enough to draw it.* The System, registering the containment, posts the line that turns the win strange: it notes the larder's lichen has been "maintained at feed-grade since the prior tenancy" — that someone *kept* this organism, on purpose, fed and bounded, and that its growth-toward-warmth is "operating as designed." The pantry was a tenant. The tenant was *placed here* to grow toward something, by someone, before her. **(mystery)**
  - And the third need stays cold. **The cost, felt on the page:** she goes up with the empty steep-cup still in her hand — she'd carried it up out of habit, or hope — and stands in the Fledgling Wing doorway where the Phoenix can see her, and sees it *see the cup,* and watches the small ash-light of it lift toward her the way it lifted toward Cinder's warmth at Ep15, expecting the thing the cup means. And she turns the cup over. Empties the nothing in it onto the floor, so it can't keep waiting on a steep that isn't coming. The Phoenix doesn't understand the gesture; it only understands that she came in and didn't help and is leaving. Liora makes herself watch it not understand. *That's* the cost — not the number on the slate, the small dropped attention of a frightened animal that trusted her to come back warm and got an empty cup and a closing door. She files it where she files the things she won't say aloud: she could have. She had the skill this morning. She chose the wall and the wing over the bird, because the wall and the wing *are* the bird's safety, and knowing that does not make the doorway any easier to stand in. The win is real and the casualty is real and they are the same hour, and she is the one who picked.
- **System box:** `> Containment: achieved (care-method, not culling). Sporeglow: bounded, feed-grade, growing toward warmth — operating as designed. Surface revealed: over-painted seam, west wall, dimensions consistent with a door. Note: the lichen was maintained, not wild. Someone fed it. Someone bounded it. Someone wanted it to grow this way, toward something kept warm behind the wall. Not you. Before you.` — the tachometer drifts a half-degree warmer *and* a half-degree stranger; the System knows the room's history and is fond of the fact.
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** The cold green lichen climbing a warmed line in the dark, and the moment it reaches the sealed seam the whole rectangle *lights* — a faint, even, breathing glow in the shape of a hidden door; the wall behind it warmer than a wall has any right to be; cellar-cold everywhere else; a single drowsy bee bumping the lit seam and settling on it like it knows.
- **Lands here:** **🍪 mystery (ON THE TURN)** — the contained lichen reveals a *kept, designed* organism and an over-painted seam with warmth behind it. **opens [L-SPORE]** (depth-4 milieu loop: *what lives behind the West Wing?* — payoff Ep30, the East Wing). **advances [L-EDDA]** (the larder was maintained by the prior tenancy; the System knows and approves — the dead carer's hand). **advances [L-SERIES]** (the warmth behind the seam is the far door, the thing the lichen was grown to point at). **Compliance 41→50%** lands here too: a hazard *contained and documented* is a legitimized operation — the triage, filed, credits the audit.
- **Exit:** *Operating as designed.* She containment a pantry and uncovered a plan — a lichen grown for a generation to point at a warm sealed door — and she did it on the morning she'd already chosen not to comfort a frightened creature. The seam glows behind her. The Phoenix shivers upstairs. Both are hers now.

## Scene 4 — Reserve insufficient for elective comfort   [S4. Disaster / cost]
- **Function / POV state:** The turn's price is paid on-page and felt — and the price is *the triage*, not the mystery. The deferred comfort is the casualty, and the System names it in deadpan so Liora doesn't have to. Cozy floor holds: the cost lands on the *way of life* and on Liora's effort, never on the Phoenix's safety (it is uncomfortable, not in danger).
- **Beats:**
  - The cost is the trade she made, now logged. She funded the two floors; the buff-steep went unspent; and the System closes the morning by stating, flatly, what she chose: that the comfort-steep was *elective*, that the reserve *funded the structural needs first*, and that a creature will stay uncomfortable for now because care, this hour, cost more than care could afford. Not a punishment — an accounting. The currency went *down* (8→7) and a frightened animal got the short end of the arithmetic, and both of those are true at once and neither is a power-fantasy.
  - Liora books the harder lesson plainly (G2): she has spent the whole nursery proving care is the structure that keeps everyone safe — and today the structure was the thing that made her leave one of them shivering, because the wards and the larder *are* the Phoenix's safety even when comforting the Phoenix directly is what her hands wanted to do. Care has a reserve and the reserve has a floor; she cannot save everything in the same hour; and the proof of it is a tremor in a corner she chose not to still. The upkeep is permanent now: Hearthglow is a leash, not a tap. Every save she funds is a save she didn't.
  - She does the one thing the reserve *will* allow, because the larder gave her it for free: she takes a little of the bounded, feed-grade sporeglow and — RC II, learned today — renders 🍲 **Sporeglow Honey**, luminous and faintly sweet, the bees having made it from the lichen along the warmed line. It is *not* a buff (no steep, no fast-track, no Hearthglow); it is ingredient-upkeep only, a thing she can give the Phoenix tonight with her hands instead of her reserve — a spoon of warm light. Comfort by labor, since comfort by currency is what she couldn't afford. It will not cure the nerves. It is what's left when the ledger says no.
  - **[VOICE-GUARD]** — resist *"some kindnesses are a debt you carry in the dark, a warmth you cannot spend made into a light you cannot keep."* Write instead: *"She couldn't afford to steep him calm. She could afford a spoonful of honey the bees made out of the larder. It wasn't enough. It was what the reserve would allow, and she gave it by hand because the hand was free."*
- **System box:** `> Reserve insufficient for elective comfort. Triage logged: ward-hold FUNDED, spore-containment FUNDED, comfort-steep DEFERRED. Hearthglow 8 → 7. Note: the deferred subject remains uncomfortable. Status: not harmed. Status: not helped. Recipe learned: Sporeglow Honey (ingredient-upkeep; not a buff; not a substitute). The System notes you gave it anyway.`
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** The reserve-tally settling one digit lower, dark; the contained lichen glowing steady and harmless behind its warmed line; up in the wing, the Phoenix taking a spoon of warm faintly-sweet light off her finger and shivering anyway — comforted a little, not cured, exactly as the ledger said it would be.
- **Lands here:** **Hearthglow 8→7** — the bite, on-page and named ("Reserve insufficient for elective comfort. Triage logged."). 🍲 **Sporeglow Honey learned** (RC II + West Wing; not a buff — the substitute she *can* afford). Upkeep booked (G3): the sporeglow is a standing containment cost (spreads if unmanaged); RC II's dependency cost is on the books; the Phoenix's chronic nerves stay flagged and *untreated by choice*. - **Exit:** She funded the house and shorted the Phoenix and gave it honey instead of a cure, and that is the best version of the morning that the arithmetic allowed. She goes to bank the night — and finds the System has filed the triage with a note she didn't write.

## Scene 5 — Sequel: maintained, not wild   [S5. Short sequel]

- **Beats:**

Everyone should have my morning."* The dry deflection over a fixed core — under it, the thing she won't say lyrically: she left the Phoenix shivering, and it will sit with her.
  - **(b) Dilemma —** stated legibly (G2): the lichen was *maintained, not wild* — fed feed-grade, bounded, grown for a generation toward a warm sealed seam, by the same prior tenancy the moonwell remembered (Ep3's *back*) and the keystone served (Ep8's watch). She can't un-know that the West Wing was *aimed* at something — and that the System calls the aiming "operating as designed," fond of a plan it expects her to finish. What is warm enough, behind that wall, to draw a lichen across a larder for years?
  - **(c) New decision —** she resolves to *let the sporeglow keep growing along the warmed line* — to manage it toward the seam instead of away from it, because a thing grown for a generation to point at a door is pointing at it for a reason, and she is done treating this house's mysteries as somebody else's filing.
