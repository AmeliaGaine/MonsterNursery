## Scene 1 — A half-degree too far   [S1. Open in motion]
- **Function / POV state:** Drop mid-action on a concrete running WANT, no scene-setting. WANT: get the morning steep onto the Brew Pool before the Toad↔Kraken frenemy dial — live since Ep9's biscuit-thieving tentacle — tips from *bickering* into *boiling*. The seam from Ep16 carries: Sorrel is in the new Mending Room down the courseway, already a standing draw on the day, which is exactly why Liora is short-handed at the pool this morning.
- **Beats:**
  - Open on Liora already mid-lunge across the steam, kettle-cloth in hand, because Cinder has waddled up onto the Brew Pool lip to cadge the warm and the Tea Kraken has taken that, as it takes everything, *personally*. The frenemy dial is running hot: toad-heat into kraken-water, a tentacle flicking the ember-toad's flank, the ember-toad flaring back. The water has gone from steeping-warm to a hard rolling fret in under a minute.
  - Diagnosis II (online @16) flags it before her hands get there — not a behaviour, a *trajectory*: water-temp climbing, one tentacle already curling toward the shallows where the morning's mugs (and Cress, drifting close to watch) are.   - She gets the cloth between toad and tentacle and lifts Cinder off the lip — Gentle Handling II, the higher-trust grip — and the rolling water *spits*, a gout of near-boil that would have caught Cress across the shallows. It doesn't. It catches the empty stone where Cress was a half-second ago.
  - **The felt beat:** then it's over, and Liora's hands won't stop. Her pool, her morning, her two hands — and she'd have scalded the calmest creature in the house. She sets Cinder down very gently, because her hands need something gentle to do, and breathes until they're hers again. The want hardens around the fear: catching this once with her own two hands is luck. The pool needs to catch it when she isn't standing here.
- **System box:** `> Brew Pool: thermal excursion logged. Cause: Ember-Toad / Tea Kraken proximity (frenemy lever, live since intake). Note: the steep is now arguing with the steeper. Current safeguard: one custodian, two hands, no margin. Disagree with the margin.`

- **Lands here:** clock live (`L-KRAKEN` foreground — the frenemy dial is hot and will tip again the next time Cinder wants the warm). Diagnosis II @16 carried in as the thing that *sees the trajectory* a half-second early.

## Scene 2 — Two hands are not a safeguard   [S2. Escalating want vs. obstacle]
- **Function / POV state:** The engine beat — give it the most room. WANT: make the Brew Pool corner safe to relax into without a custodian standing over it. The obstacle is *a frenemy dial that answers every fix by finding the gap in it*, and every try costs more.
- **Beats:**
  - **Try 1 (separate them — fails the right way):** Liora's first instinct is the clerk's instinct: remove the variable. Move Cinder's basking off the pool lip, give the Kraken its water back. She does it well. It holds until Cinder, who runs hot and likes the warm and is *four*, waddles straight back the moment her back is turned, because you cannot zone a toddler out of the one warm spot by drawing a line on the floor. First cost: an hour, and the dawning that separation isn't care, it's just culling with extra steps.
  - **Try 2 (cool the steep — fails harder):** She tries the cooking lever — Resonance Cooking I @9, the cooler steep, the soothing leaf-blend that takes the Kraken's edge off. It works on the *Kraken*. It does nothing for the *toad*, who isn't in the tea, and the next proximity flare proves it: the dial isn't about the steep, it's about two creatures who provoke each other being able to *reach* each other across an unguarded margin. Second cost: a measure of Hearthglow spent on a soothe that addressed half the problem, and Diagnosis II flagging — *chronic, not acute; this recurs.* (S8's sting: finer sight catches the condition that won't take a one-time fix.)
  - **Try 3 — costs most (name what the corner actually needs):** She stops treating the creatures and reads the *room.* What the Brew Pool lacks isn't a rule or a recipe — it's a **ward**: a standing thermal margin built into the stone itself, a thing that holds the half-second so her hands don't have to. The grief is dry and specific and it sits in the room — Sorrel is not a manual; she is a person who carried a loss here and is handing Liora the thing that prevents it. The cost of Try 3 is that it points at a skill Liora has been circling all arc and hasn't yet had: this is a Ward-Mending job, and she's about to find out what wards cost.

## Scene 4 — The tap she can't turn off   [S4. Disaster / cost]
- **Function / POV state:** The turn's price is paid on-page and felt. The win is real; the cost is the leash it just clipped on — a standing per-episode drain booked as upkeep (G3). - **Beats:**
