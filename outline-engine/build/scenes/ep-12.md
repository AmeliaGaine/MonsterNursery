## Scene 2 — The checklist that was written to fail   [S2. Escalating want vs. obstacle]
- **Function / POV state:** The engine beat — most room here. WANT: walk Quill through a real working house and let the *system of care* answer the form. The obstacle hardens with every line: the checklist was authored by the fear-economy, so each box is a trap, and Quill raises the bar each time Liora clears one.
- **Beats:**
  - **Try 1 — Containment.** Quill's first heading is "containment of scheduled creatures." She expects cages. Liora gives her the Basking Nook: Cinder banked warm, fire-safety upkeep logged, *heat is not harm* rendered as architecture, not restraint. Quill writes something. Then she raises the bar: containment under *stress* — she raps the Nook wall to see the toad spook. Cinder doesn't combust; he eats. First box cleared by method, not luck — and the cost is Liora's pulse, because that rap could have gone wrong and the floor is heat ≤4: nobody harmed, but the *risk* was real and felt.
  - **Try 2 — Distress.** Quill's column reads "creature distress / public hazard." She wants to find a frightened animal. Liora uses **Diagnosis I** (online since Ep7) out loud — names what each creature needs before Quill can call it a hazard: Cress's moonlight ration, Cinder's thermal floor, Bertram's load-bearing sulk-risk. The competence is in *seeing the hurt before it shows.* Quill, who has only ever read these creatures as threats, has no column for a custodian who diagnoses care. She raises the bar again: show her the *worst* one. Show her the cockatrice, the kraken, the thing that screams. (Liora gives her the Kraken, steeping — see Scene 3.)
  - **Try 3 (`[IDLE-TICK]` lands here) — Handling.** Quill demands a handling demonstration of a creature at real trust — the box most premises fail, because trust can't be faked under a clipboard. Liora handles Cress with **Gentle Handling II** (online since Ep11): the feeding only Cress's hands-of-choice can give. Mid-demonstration the System posts its quiet idle line — the compliance number ticking *as she works*, the care being scored in real time. - **System box (idle-tick):**

- **Sensory / atmosphere:** Clipboard pages flicking against the snowmelt-light; Cinder's ember-warmth pushing back the draught; the Kraken's steam threading up while Quill's pen scratches; the dry tick of a number climbing in a house that was illegal an hour ago.

## Scene 3 — The box the form keeps empty   [S3. The yes-but turn · COOKIE]
- **Function / POV state:** THE TURN. The cookie (competence-display) and the complication land in the same beat (yes-but). WANT met: clear the unclearable box by letting the *care-system itself* — not Liora's argument — be the proof.
- **Beats:**

  - **[STAGED — wonder, concrete sensory amid the climax]:** And the room, for one held minute, simply *works* in a way you can see and smell and feel: Cinder's heat and the Kraken's steep meet in the middle of the air and the cold inspection-light goes soft and gold with it; the bergamot rises, the snowmelt ticks off the gutter in even time with Cress's drip, and the frost on the cleared windows lets go all at once, sheeting down the glass so the thin sun comes through warm for the first time and lays a bright bar across the signed page.
