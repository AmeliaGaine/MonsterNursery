## Scene 1 — The town at the gate   [KI — cozy status-quo + small want]
- **Function / POV state:** Establish the warm, working midsummer status-quo of a Briarwick that has *privately* proved its thesis (the Pebble Garden laid, the chick warm and dropping path-pebbles for the family) — and a modest, legible want with no threat in it. Liora's want entering: not to *win the town over* (too big, not today) — just to let the people at the gate *see the true thing* and go home with it. A small, clean intention: show them what actually happens when the cockatrice "strikes," because the truth is gentler than the rumor and she'd rather they had the truth. No spectacle planned; just an honest demonstration of a chore she does every morning anyway.
- **Beats:**
  - A bright open midsummer noon. Word has got round the valley — Briarwick is *keeping a deadly cockatrice* — and a knot of townsfolk has gathered at the garden gate, not hostile exactly, but wary, kept-back, watching from the safe side of the wall the way you watch a fire someone swears is under control. The wind moves through the trees beyond the garden in long soft passes, like distant surf, and under it the crowd is very quiet. They came to see a monster. The garden, infuriatingly, is just warm stone and long grass and a chick the size of a teapot sunning itself on a flat rock.
  - The want lands small, real, and dry: Liora doesn't intend to *convert* anyone — she's a clerk, not a preacher, and a crowd is not a form she can file. She intends only to do the ordinary thing in the open: handle the chick the way she handles it every day, let its famous death-glare land on her the way it lands on Bertram every morning, and let the watchers see that the "strike" produces a *warm pebble*, not a corpse. The truth, demonstrated. If they take it home, good. If they don't — she still has a chick to tend, and the tending doesn't pause for an audience.
  - But the open view changes the math, and she feels it before she can name it. Handling the chick in front of the family is one thing; handling it where forty wary strangers are counting is another. She reads the chick (Diagnosis I/II @7/@16): it's steady now, sunning, calm — but a chick that's never been *watched* like this might not stay calm, and a startled cockatrice doesn't get more dangerous (it can't; that's the whole point) so much as *less convincing* — a flinch, a missed pebble, a chick that bolts under the rock would read, to this crowd, as the monster proving them right. The handling has to be *clean*, or the rumor wins. She files the new weight: today, a fumble is not hers to absorb privately. It belongs to forty witnesses and the valley they'll tell.
- **System box:** deadpan Custodian Note, unbothered, the new public-exposure stated flat the moment she steps to the gate: *"Gentle Handling: tier III available. Conditions: subject 'deadly' (per public record), audience present (unscheduled, ~40). Note: a fumble is no longer a private cost. New upkeep: public liability. Recommendation: handle as usual. The usual is sufficient. They are not."*
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** 🌦️ bright open midsummer noon; the warm flat stones of the Pebble Garden underfoot; long grass past the new low wall going to seed in the heat; the wind through the far trees like distant surf, rising and falling; the crowd's held breath a kind of silence you can hear; the chick small and unbothered on its sunning rock, blinking. Dry register — the stakes rendered as a clerk noticing the audit just got witnesses, not as dread.

## Scene 2 — The handling, in plain sun   [SHŌ — gentle development / process · the ASMR zone]

- **Beats:**
