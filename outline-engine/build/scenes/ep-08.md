## Scene 1 — A corner that wasn't sagging yesterday   [S1. Open in motion]
- **Function / POV state:** Drop mid-action on a concrete running WANT, no scene-setting. WANT: find why the morning's Habitat-Stability number went *down* overnight when nothing was touched — and put it back. The seam from Ep7 carries: Diagnosis I (online @7) now means Liora *sees* every untreated hurt, and the first hurt it shows her this morning isn't a creature — it's the building.
- **Beats:**
  - Open on Liora already on the back stair with the level and the lamp, already chasing a draught that's new since last night, because the Stability tally posted lower at dawn than it read when she banked it. She mended this wing three days ago (Hab Mending I @5). Mended rooms stay mended. This one didn't.
  - She finds the northeast corner gone soft — a hand's-width of settle in a wall that was true, the plaster crazed, the joint opened just enough that wind worries it and the night's rain has found the gap. Diagnosis I flags it without being asked: *untreated; load-bearing; getting worse.*
  - She looks up to the cold perch over the corner — Bertram's perch, the squat chunky weight that has sat that keystone since before she had the green key — and it is *empty*. Moss is already greening the stone where he isn't. He's blinked off post. The want hardens: this isn't a mending job, it's a *missing gargoyle* job.
- **System box:** `> Habitat Stability: −. Cause: structural, northeast keystone. Note: load-bearing element absent from designated post. This is not subsidence. This is a vacancy.`
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** Grey morning wind worrying the loose corner like a tongue at a sore tooth; rain has run the night's length down the inside of the sag; the perch-stone cold and already going green where a warm weight used to sit.
- **Lands here:** clock live (`L-STAB` foreground — the corner is sagging and will keep sagging until the keystone is back). Diagnosis I @7 carried in as the thing that *shows* her the hurt.
- **Exit:** She goes to find where a building's own gargoyle goes when he doesn't want to be a building. The level stays on the sill; this won't be fixed with a level.

## Scene 2 — Mm.   [S2. Escalating want vs. obstacle]
- **Function / POV state:** The engine beat — give it the most room. WANT: get Bertram back on his post before the sag costs the wing. The obstacle is *a sulking architectural spirit who has decided, today, not to be load-bearing*, and every try answers back harder.
- **Beats:**
  - **Try 1 (the practical fix fails the right way):** Liora's first instinct is her trade's instinct — treat the *symptom*. Re-wad the joint, jack the sill, true the corner the way Ep5 taught her. She does it well. It holds for an hour and settles again, because the corner isn't broken — it's *unoccupied*. You cannot mend a wall back to a weight that chose to leave. First cost: an hour and a measure of Hearthglow spent on a fix that was never going to take.
  - **Try 2 (find him):** She blink-tracks him — Bertram surfaces, squat and chunky, in the absolute wrong places: wedged in the cold larder, perched glowering on the rain-barrel, once squarely in the middle of the Basking Nook where his stone goes *tick* in Cinder's heat. Each time she reaches him he blinks again. He is not lost. He is *avoiding*. When she finally pins him on the wash-house roof and asks, plainly, what's wrong, she gets the whole of his vocabulary: **"Mm."** Flat. Unhappy. Final.
    - **[STAGED — landed Bertram/System dry gag]:** The System, tracking the keystone's wanderings all morning, has been quietly amending its own records, and posts the running tally without being asked: `> Load-bearing element last confirmed at designated post: 14h ago. Subsequent confirmed locations: larder (cold). Rain-barrel (wet). Basking Nook (occupied; complaint filed by Ember-Toad). Wash-house roof (current). Note: structurally, the building is being held up by spite. This is within tolerance.` She reads it on the roof in the rain and concedes, privately, that spite has held up worse buildings than this.
  - **Try 3 — costs most (read the sulk):** She stops chasing and *diagnoses* (Diagnosis I, used on a creature for once, not a wall). She walks it back: the new patron at the Welcome Desk (Ep5) on the book; the warm pair at the moonwell the System keeps addressing by name; the whole house lately arranged around arrivals and creatures and *her*. Bertram has held this keystone since before any of them. And lately nobody has so much as said the corner's name. The sulk isn't structural. It's **"this place is mine, and nobody remembered."** The cost of seeing it is that she can't unsee how long he's gone unthanked.
- **System box:** **[IDLE-TICK]** mid-try — `> Hearthglow: +1 (reduced distress, in progress). Reserve 6 → 7. Note: the keystone is not malfunctioning. The keystone has feelings. This was always in the manual.`
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** Rain off and on all morning; the wash-house slates slick under a glowering stone weight; the *tick* of cold gargoyle in a warm room; "Mm." landing like a door not quite shutting.
- **Lands here:** **Hearthglow 6→7** (the idle-tick, in-register — reduced distress earns it even mid-problem). `L-STAB` clock tightens: the corner is still sagging while she works out *why*.
- **Exit:** She has the diagnosis and no procedure for it. Gentle Handling fixes a flinch; there is no skill on the tree for *apologizing to a building.* She'll have to do it the only way that ever works here — by hand, and meaning it.

## Scene 3 — The keystone is not a part. It's a resident.   [S3. The yes-but turn · COOKIE LANDS HERE]
- **Function / POV state:** THE TURN. The cookie and the complication arrive in the same beat (yes-but). WANT met: get Bertram back on post — not by mending, by *belonging*. But the win recontextualizes the whole house, and the recontextualization is the mystery.
- **Beats:**
  - Liora goes back to the cold perch and does the un-clerklike thing: she *tends the post itself*. Scrapes the moss, warms the stone with a cloth off Cinder's heat, sets the corner right under it — and then, out loud, flat, she names it as his: not a load-bearing element, not a Stability line-item, but Bertram's, and overdue a thank-you. She invites the weight back, instead of jacking the wall up to meet a hole. Bertram blinks onto the warmed stone, settles his bulk into the keystone seat — and the sag *closes* under him, the wall true again, held by a resident, not a repair. **"Mm."** — and this one means *yes.*
  - **Yes-but (the cookie lands on the same beat):** the corner holds — **BUT** the System, registering the keystone re-seated, posts the line that turns the win strange. It doesn't say *element restored*. It says Bertram has held this keystone *since before the last custodian* — names the post as older than the green key, older than the redacted name — and notes, too specifically to be procedural, that **he has been waiting on this seat for someone to come back and keep it warm.** The fix is real. The mystery is that the gargoyle was *left guarding a post*, by someone, for a return — and the System is fond of him in a register a system shouldn't have. **(mystery)**
  - Liora gets the rule the whole episode was teaching while she ran: this gain *reversed* — the Stability she'd banked came undone the moment the weight that held it withdrew. Nothing here stays won by being filed. It stays won by being *kept warm.*
- **System box:** `> Keystone: re-seated. Habitat Stability: recovering. Note: Blink Gargoyle (designation: Bertram) — load-bearing since before the current rota. Held post through the vacancy. Held it for you, specifically. Recommend you do not make him do it twice.` — the sting on *for you, specifically*; the tachometer drifts a half-degree warmer and a half-degree stranger.
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** The wind stops finding the corner — the house goes a half-degree quieter where it had been worrying; the warmed perch-stone steaming faintly in the cold; a squat weight settling with a sound like a building exhaling.
- **Lands here:** **🍪 mystery (ON THE TURN)** · **opens [L-STAB]** (the keystone is now a named, load-bearing relationship — depth-3 loop, payoff Ep44 when Bertram *holds* through the seizure) · **advances [L-EDDA]** (the System knows Bertram's tenure predates the redacted custodian, and is fond of him on purpose). Bertram ~30→40%; the "this place is mine" easing begins.
- **Exit:** *Held it for you, specifically.* Liora didn't ask to be a *you* anyone held a post for. The word sits wrong, the same wrong as *back* did at the moonwell, and it won't leave.

## Scene 4 — The gain that came undone   [S4. Disaster / cost]
- **Function / POV state:** The turn's price is paid on-page and felt. The win is real; the cost is the rule it taught — and a new standing fragility booked as upkeep (G3). Cozy floor holds: the cost lands on the *house* and the *way of life*, never on Bertram.
- **Beats:**
  - The cost is the mechanic itself, now formal: Bertram is the keystone, which means the keystone has *moods.* Every line of Habitat Stability that runs through that corner is leashed to a creature's contentment — sulk, and he blinks off post; off post, and the corner sags; sagged, and the number she banked drops. The Ledger writes it as standing upkeep: she can no longer treat Stability as a fixed asset. **Your brick has moods.**
  - Liora books the harder lesson plainly (G2): this is the first time a gain has *reversed.* Comfort she could lose, sure — but Stability felt structural, *done*, the kind of number that only goes up. It went down overnight because she stopped paying attention to the one resident who never asks for anything. Every gain in this house adds an upkeep; this one added the upkeep of *noticing.*
  - **[VOICE-GUARD]** — resist *"some foundations are made of being remembered, and forgetting is its own slow collapse."* Write instead: *"She'd mended the wall twice. The wall was never the problem. The problem was that she'd forgotten to say thank you to the only thing holding it up."*
- **System box:** `> Habitat-Stability mechanic: formalized. Keystone element is a resident, not a fixture. Upkeep: his contentment is now your load-bearing concern. Note: gains in this house are not deposits. They are kept, or they are not.`
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** The corner true again, plaster drying pale where it had been dark with rain; the moss she scraped drying to nothing on the sill; the lamp guttering low on a morning that should have been a clean win and instead booked a permanent new worry.
- **Lands here:** **Hab recovers to 24%** — re-earned by belonging, not by the level — *but the gain is now revocable* (the mechanic's cost). Upkeep booked: Bertram's contentment is standing Habitat-Stability upkeep, G3. The reader has learned, for the first time, that the numbers can go *down.*
- **Exit:** She looks from the re-seated keystone to the rest of the house — every other resident the System addresses by name — and a colder question opens than the one she fixed.
