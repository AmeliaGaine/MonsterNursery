## Scene 2 — On the staged path   [S2. Escalating want vs. obstacle]
- **Function / POV state:** The engine beat — give it the most room. WANT: turn a viewing built to frighten into a viewing that *fails to frighten*, in public, scored, on Control's ground. The obstacle hardens with each try, because the crowd came pre-loaded and Grindle's man is working it: every calm thing Liora does, the stagecraft reframes as a trick, a muzzle, a cover-up. Three rising tries, each costing more standing.
- **Beats:**
  - **Try 1 — present the creature plainly (and the framing answers harder):** Liora sets the cockatrice on the path in the open noon, no cage, no muzzle, nothing hidden — the most honest thing she can do. But honesty reads as bravado to a primed crowd, and Grindle's clerk narrates it as recklessness: *see how she handles it bare, see how close she lets it to your children.* The plainness she meant as proof is reframed as proof of danger. The crowd leans back. Public Handling's cost lands for real: a private handling is judged on whether the creature is safe; a *public* one is judged on whether the audience *feels* safe — and they have been told all morning that they shouldn't.
  - **Try 2 — let the menace happen on purpose (and it's read as a stunt):** She does the brave, counterintuitive thing — she lets the cockatrice do the very thing they fear: she invites the death-glare, in the open, at herself. The chick fixes, strains, *petrifies* — and fails, the way it always fails, dropping two warm pebbles at her feet instead of turning her to stone. The thesis made visible: the menace fails into pebbles. But the clerk is ready for it, and reframes the failure as a rehearsed act — *a trained trick, a tame display, ask her to do it with a real one* — and a ripple of doubt goes through the crowd that's worse than fear, because doubt can be sold longer. **[IDLE-TICK]** the System posts the day's Renown line mid-event, scored and unbothered: *the viewing is being counted, in real time, by people who came to be afraid.*
  - **Try 3 — the move that costs the most (hand the fear to them):** She sees that nothing she *demonstrates* will land while she's the one holding it — every proof she performs, the staging can call a trick. The only thing the staging can't reframe is a thing the *crowd does with its own hands*. So she does the expensive thing: she stops performing safety and offers it — she picks up a warm pebble the failed glare just dropped, still holding the day's heat, and holds it out, flat on her palm, toward the front row. Not a demonstration. An *offer*. Come and take the deadly thing's leavings; come and feel that it's just a warm stone; come and find out for yourself, where I can't be accused of tricking you, because the proof will be in your own pocket. It costs her the most because it surrenders control of the outcome: she cannot make a single one of them take it. She can only hold out her hand in the staged noon and wait to see if anybody will.
- **System box:** **[IDLE-TICK]** mid-event — `> Public viewing: in progress. Renown: scoring live. Current crowd disposition: afraid, then doubtful (doubt logged as the more durable product). Note: every proof you perform is being reframed as a performance. Recommendation withheld. The System observes that the only evidence this crowd will trust is the evidence they hold in their own hand. The System mentions this and then, uncharacteristically, says nothing else.`
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** White noon hammering the path, no shade, heat coming up through the soles; the cockatrice blinking placid in the worst possible light; two warm pebbles bright on the dust where the failed glare dropped them; the crowd a wall of sun-squinting faces leaning fractionally back; Grindle's clerk's voice smooth and carrying; and Liora's own hand, palm up, a single warm stone on it, held out into the gap between the staging and the people, the heat off the path the loudest thing for a long, held second.
- **Lands here:** **`L-FEAR` advances toward its turn** (the foreground clock fully built — Control's staged fear vs. one held-out pebble; the loop is poised). The Public Handling cost is fully felt: scored in real time, staged by the apparatus, every demonstration reframable as a trick — the new standing problem rendered on-page. No stat resolves yet; the cost is the *exposure*, paid into the turn. Cozy floor holds: nobody harmed; the cockatrice fails safely on cue; the menace is the *framing*, not the creature.
- **Exit:** Her hand is out, a warm stone on it, in front of a crowd built to flinch. She has done everything she can do. Whether the loop turns is now, for the first time, not hers to decide — it's theirs.

## Scene 3 — One pocketed pebble   [S3. The yes-but turn · COOKIE LANDS HERE]
- **Function / POV state:** THE TURN. The cookie (competence) and the complication land on the same beat (yes-but). WANT met: the held-out proof *works* — but not the way a triumph works, and the win arrives fused to the cost that fires the rest of the arc. The competence on display is the whole-shaped one: she didn't out-argue the staging, she *out-cared* it, by handing the fear to the people who held it.
- **Beats:**
  - The held second breaks. For a long moment nobody moves, and the clerk's voice starts to fill the silence with the next reframe — and then one townsperson steps out of the front row. Not a believer, not an ally — a wary, sun-squinting ordinary person who came to be afraid like everyone else — and reaches out, and takes the pebble off Liora's palm. Feels it. A warm stone, just a warm stone, the heat of an ordinary noon in it and nothing else. The crowd watches one of their own *not* be turned to stone, *not* be harmed, hold the deadly thing's leavings in a bare hand and find it warm. The staging has no reframe for this, because it isn't Liora's hand anymore.
  - **The competence cookie lands here (the whole shape of it):** this is the skill made visible — Public Handling not as performing safety but as *transferring* it; Gentle Handling III's quiet certainty (@27) turned outward so that the proof ends up in a stranger's pocket where no clerk can call it a trick. Liora names the failed petrification out loud, plainly, for the record and the crowd: *it tries; it fails; this is what the failure makes* — and the pebble in the townsperson's hand is the footnote nobody can argue with. The competence is not winning the room. It is making *one* person able to know a true thing with their own hand. **(competence — ON THE TURN.)** The System scores it: Renown ticks, and for the first time the tick is *public*, logged in front of witnesses.
  - **Yes-but (the cookie's price, same beat):** it works — *and* the townsperson, instead of putting the pebble down when the viewing ends, instead of handing it back, *pockets* it. Carries it home. Which is the warm advance of `L-FEAR` (the smallest true turn — one person softened, one pebble gone home in a pocket) *and* the precise, double-edged cost of Public Handling arriving in the same motion: she has, in front of Control's own staged crowd, *publicly* changed one mind — and a public figure who can change minds in a staged room is exactly the figure the apparatus most needs to stage *again*, harder, somewhere she can't hand the proof away. The win is real and it is *evidence against her*: she has proven she's good at this where Grindle was watching, and being good at this in public is the thing that guarantees the next, bigger room. A pocketed pebble is a victory she'll be billed for.
- **System box:** `> Public viewing: concluded. Outcome: one (1) townsperson took the pebble and did not return it. Failed petrification: named on record. Renown: +tick (logged publicly; witnessed). L-FEAR: advanced — disposition of one resident shifted from afraid to *carrying-it-home.* Bond — Cockatrice: +tick (handled, in public, and trusted to fail safely in front of strangers). Note: you changed a mind where Control was counting. The System congratulates you. The System also notes that Control was counting, and now knows precisely how good at this you are.` — the tachometer drifts a half-degree warmer at *carrying-it-home*, then catches itself on the last line.
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** The white noon unchanged but the held breath let go; one ordinary hand closing warm around a warm stone; the crowd's lean-back gone slack, uncertain, *thinking*; the clerk's smooth narration finding nothing to say; the cockatrice blinking placid in the heat, oblivious to having just been the most persuasive thing on the path; and one figure at the front turning to go home with a deadly thing's pebble warming in a pocket against a thigh.

## Scene 4 — Scored from now on   [S4. Disaster / cost]

- **Beats:**

Today's viewing was the first; it will not be the last, because today *worked*, and a thing that works for her is a thing the apparatus will restage on worse ground until it works for them. Every appearance from here is scored — and a scored thing can be *engineered to lose*. She has not won free of the staging. She has proven worth staging.
  - The cost of being good in public, named: the pocketed pebble is the warmest thing that happened all day and also the most expensive, because Grindle watched one stranger change their mind, and Grindle's whole trade is knowing the price of a changed mind. She has shown her hand. The next room will not have warm pebbles she's allowed to hand out; the next room will be built so the proof can't leave her palm. Competence in public buys you the obligation to be competent in public *again,* under conditions chosen by people who want you to fail.

Her care can move it up; Control's staging can move it down; and from today every appearance is a beat on a board she no longer solely owns.
