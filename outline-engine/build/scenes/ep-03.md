## Scene 1 — The feeding hour   [S1 · open in motion]
- **Function / POV state:** Drop mid-action on a concrete running WANT — get the mooncalf fed before the morning compliance window closes. Liora wants the simple thing: a creature eating, a number she can log, a morning that behaves.
- **Beats:**
  - Open on Liora already at the moonwell with the warmed mineral-bowl, already on her third attempt of the hour; Cress has turned its face to the wall again. No throat-clearing — the bowl is going cold in her hands.
  - She runs the Gentle Handling I steps by the book (the only handling she has): low approach, offered palm, patience. Cress leans away from the bowl the moment she sets it down and someone — anyone — else's shadow falls near.
  - Carry-in from Ep2: Cinder (intake @2) is wedged warm against the hearthbrick; the new pair *should* be settling. Cress isn't.
- **System box (if any):** *"Feeding 3 of 3, Lunar Mooncalf. Intake: 0 g. Note: appetite present. Obstruction: unlisted."* — deadpan, with the editorial sting on *unlisted*.
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** Grey late-winter light at the panes; breath-fog at the feeding hour; the well drips a slow cold metronome that keeps time over the whole scene.
- **Lands here:** clock live (`L-TRUST` foreground — Cress must eat).
- **Exit:** Liora hands the bowl to Fen to try, because surely it's the hand, not the food. Cut to the test.

## Scene 2 — Every hand but hers   [S2 · escalating want vs. obstacle]
- **Function / POV state:** The want meets a hardening obstacle; Liora applies competence; the obstacle answers back harder. Three rising tries, each costing more.
- **Beats:**
  - **Try 1:** Fen offers the same bowl, same steps. Cress refuses flat — not frightened, just *closed*. Liora reasons it's the apprentice's nerves.
  - **Try 2:** She has Fen mimic her exact posture, her exact approach; she even warms the bowl on Cinder's brick so the food is identical. Still nothing. The variable isn't the food, the temperature, or the method — she's controlled for all three. **(staged friction — Cinder × ledger, deadpan):** A re-warming spills; a thread of grey mineral-milk runs off the bench and pools on the open feeding-ledger. Cinder, who has decided the puddle is *his problem to solve*, levers off his brick and settles his full radiant weight squarely onto the wet column to dry it. He is correct: the puddle dries. The receipt under him curls up at both corners like a snail withdrawing, and the morning's entries cook to a brown crisp. Liora looks at the ruined column, then at the toad, who is plainly waiting to be thanked. *"That was the only legible copy,"* she tells him. He does not move. The day's intake is now warm, dry, and unreadable.
  - **Try 3 (costs most):** She tries the cold logic of her old trade — leave the bowl, withdraw, let hunger decide. Cress will not eat unattended either. It waits. The morning compliance window is now closing; the unfed-log is about to cost her.
- **System box:** **[IDLE-TICK]** mid-try — *"Compliance: +1. Procedure followed correctly. Outcome noted as anomalous, not deficient. Ledger legibility: 1 column lost to occupant initiative. Filed as care."* (the small non-foreground tick; Compliance ticks even on a failing morning; the editorial sting lands on the curled receipt).
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** The fog thins; the drip keeps counting; the bowl has been re-warmed so many times the mineral-milk has gone grey at the skin; one ledger column dried to a brown curl and a toad refusing to apologize for it.
- **Lands here:** Compliance +1 (idle-tick). `L-TRUST` clock tightens.
- **Exit:** Liora takes the bowl back herself, out of stubbornness more than hope — and Cress turns *toward* her. The variable was never the method.

## Scene 3 — Her hands, and a note addressed   [S3 · the yes-but turn · COOKIE LANDS HERE]
- **Function / POV state:** The hinge. She gets the win — *but* it triggers a worse thing. The competence-display and the new complication arrive in the same beat.
- **Beats:**
  - Liora kneels, offers the bowl one more time with her own hands, runs Gentle Handling I clean — and Cress eats. Fully. Calmly. First feeding accepted. The care-method visibly *worked*: it was her, specifically, all along. **(competence-display)**
  - **Yes-but:** the relief curdles into a question. *Why her?* She's no one — a defected clerk three weeks in the door. Before she can shelve the thought, the System posts the feeding confirmation — and it is **addressed to her by something more than a job title**, in a register too specific to be procedural.
  - The note reads as if the System already *knew* the mooncalf would only take from this pair of hands — as if it had been waiting for the right hands.
- **System box:** *"Feeding accepted. Lunar Mooncalf stabilizing. Note: the well always knew which hands. Welcome back to the rota."* — the sting on *back*. (Tachometer: the deadpan drifts a half-degree warmer, and a half-degree stranger.)
- **Sensory / atmosphere:** The drip slows as Cress settles; for one beat the cold well goes still and the room is only breathing and the small sound of a creature finally eating.
- **Lands here:** **🍪 competence (@turn)** · **closes [L-TRUST]** (Cress 20→30%, trust threshold crossed) · the first thread of **[L-HEIR]** surfaces in the note.
- **Exit:** *Welcome back.* Liora hasn't been here before. The word sits wrong, and won't leave.

## Scene 5 — Sequel: the name nobody wrote down   [S5 · short sequel]
- **Function / POV state:** The de-escalation valve, kept lean. Reaction → dilemma → the decision that fires the next chapters.
- **Beats:**

  - **(b) dilemma —** stated plainly (G2): the System didn't *learn* to trust her — it *recognized* her, the way a ledger recognizes a returning name. Either it's malfunctioning, or she stepped into a chair that already had a nameplate scraped off it.
