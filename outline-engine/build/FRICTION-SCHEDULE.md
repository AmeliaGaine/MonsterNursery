# FRICTION-SCHEDULE.md — Per-Episode Creature Friction & Synergy

*The cast-chemistry engine, scheduled. Every episode gets one concrete staged beat drawn
from `cast-synergy-map.md`, weighted so FRICTION lands on the 23 humor-breach episodes
flagged in `AXIS-ENVELOPE.md` and the fosters' best gags front-load into Arcs 1–3 before
they depart. Arc 4 humor is carried entirely by the PERMANENT CORE.*

## Legend (4 lines)
- **FRICTION** = anti-synergy / frenemy beat → HUMOR. A specific staged gag, never "they interact."
- **SYNERGY** = a synergy-edge beat → COZY warmth. A ready-made chapter-warm-out.
- **Availability respected:** a creature's beat appears only in episodes where it is present (no departed/not-yet-arrived assignments). Cinder ≤33 · Slime ≤20 · Kettle ≤41 · Kraken 9+ · fosters 13+ · Corehound 22+ · Cockatrice 26+.
- **Tone 8, cozy-contained:** chaos threatens teacups / receipts / dignity, never the safe place. Grief/climax eps (34,36,37,45,46,47) get a single DRY beat that lifts humor to ~4 without breaking tone.

## The three RUNNING GAGS (escalate across the book)
1. **THE BISCUIT TRIBUNAL** (Kraken × Corehound): a tentacle surfaces in the pot and steals a biscuit; the Corehound's food-head opens a one-dog inquest while the other two heads abstain. Escalates from theft → formal grievance → a standing "tribunal" the house humors. *Advances: Ep23 (seed), 32, 38, 43, 44, 48.*
2. **BERTRAM'S PUDDLE "Mm."** (Slime × Gargoyle, then Gargoyle solo): Bertram blinks across the room and lands squarely in a fresh puddle, says "Mm.", does not move. After Slime departs the gag survives as Bertram's one-syllable verdict on any indignity (a cockatrice glare, a sag, a storm). *Advances: Ep8, 15, 20, 26, 31, 44, 46.*
3. **THE THREE-WAY VOTE** (Corehound-internal): three heads, three thermostats, zero agreement — alarm-head, sulk-head, already-forgiven-head deadlock on everything from bed temperature to whether to be alarmed. Escalates from blankets → biscuit verdicts → the Arc-4 inspection where the deadlock finally resolves into one warning. *Advances: Ep22 (seed), 32, 39, 42, 44, 48.*

## Arc 4 durability note
The fosters carry the heaviest, most physical friction (Toad×Slime, Shriekmoth feedback loops,
Phoenix-ash) and are spent down across Arcs 1–3 — Slime's gag retires at Ep20, Cinder's frenemy
at Ep33, Kettle's shriek converts to a chime and exits at Ep41. To prevent a humor cliff, all
three RUNNING GAGS are owned by PERMANENT CORE creatures and are deliberately seeded early
(biscuit tribunal Ep9/23, three-way vote Ep22, puddle "Mm." Ep6/8) so they are fully warmed up
before Arc 4. Arc 4 (Ep38–49) then runs almost entirely on core friction: the **biscuit
tribunal** (38,43,44,45,48), the **Corehound three-way vote** (39,42,44,47,48), **Gargoyle×Cockatrice
"two stones"** (38,42,43,46,49), **Cockatrice×Phoenix** (49), and Bertram's **"Mm."** (44,46). Every
Arc-4 episode lands at least one core-driven beat, and three of the gags pay off their long arc
exactly at the climax (the vote resolving into one true warning Ep44; "Mm." decoded as Edda's
keeper Ep46; the tribunal closing warm Ep48) — so the humor floor is held by payoff, not padding,
right through the finale. Grief/climax eps (34,36,37,45,46,47) carry a single dry beat each,
lifting humor to ~4 without breaking the cozy-contained tone.
