# MICRO-TEMPLATES.md — Stage 3.4 (Micro-Structure Designer)

*The two reusable chapter blueprints the beat-generators fill. Each is a slot-grid for
a single ~2,000–2,800-word episode. CADENCE.md (3.3) decides WHICH template a chapter
uses and tags its cookie/out; LOOPMAP.md (3.2) supplies WHICH loops open/advance/close;
STATSPINE.md (3.5) supplies WHICH stat/skill numbers are legal to move. These templates
supply the **shape** — where the beats land, where the cookie lands, where the out lands,
how the voice is held. Honors CANON.md verbatim and all six GUARDRAILS. Target mix per
3.3: ~60% KISHŌTENKETSU / ~40% SCENE&SEQUEL; **every arc climax is SCENE&SEQUEL.***

*Citation keys match CANON.md: C1/C2 = manuscript chapters; ARCH = ARCHITECTURE.md;
G1–G6 = GUARDRAILS.md.*

---

## 0 — SHARED LAW (both templates obey)

These hold regardless of which template a chapter runs.

- **Closed Loop, Open Door (ARCH 2E.3):** every episode CLOSES or advances ≥1 loop AND
  CRACKS one door one size larger. The template's "out" beat IS the open door.
- **Cookie Law (G4):** ≥1 cookie of {number-up · mystery clue · relationship beat ·
  landed joke · competence-display}. Adjacent chapters never repeat the same cookie type
  (CADENCE.md polices the run; the template just marks the **slot** the cookie lands in).
- **Anti-plateau (G5):** a live clock OR deepening mystery is visibly ticking on-page.
  Both templates carry a mandatory **idle-tick slot** (one small System line, one stat
  nudge, one voice-drift) so nothing freezes — even cozy chapters.
- **The tachometer (ARCH 2E.3):** the System's deadpan drifts warmer by tiny increments
  as Bond/Hearthglow rise. The voice's tone IS the meter; never print a mystery-% bar.
- **Stat legality (G3):** no beat wins on an ability/room not yet unlocked in STATSPINE.md.
  Every gain books a matching upkeep line in the same episode.
- **Lane floor (G1/G6):** heat ≤ 4. No loved creature harmed on-page; fosters are placed,
  never lost. The chapter is safe-for-now, warmly, by its last line.

### Cookie/out/loop TAG GLOSSARY (used in the worked examples below)

| Tag | Means |
|---|---|
| `[COOKIE:type]` | the cookie payment lands here (type ∈ number-up/mystery/relationship/joke/competence) |
| `[LOOP:ID open/advance/close]` | a LOOPMAP loop moves here |
| `[IDLE-TICK]` | the mandatory small non-foreground advance (G5 insurance) |
| `[OUT:hook]` / `[OUT:warm]` | the chapter's ending mode — hook-out (SCENE&SEQUEL) / warm-out (KISHŌTENKETSU) |
| `[VOICE-GUARD]` | a spot where prose is most tempted toward lyric; the guard fires here |

## 1 — SCENE & SEQUEL TEMPLATE  (the ~40% tension chapters)

> Use for: pressure chapters, all four arc climaxes (ARCH 2E.4), any episode where a
> live clock (inspection, thermal spike, sagging wall, a foster who must be placed) is
> the foreground. Out = **hook** (soft or hard, never two cruel hooks adjacent, G6).

### 1.1 — Beat skeleton (word budget for a 2,000–2,800-word episode)

| # | Beat | What it does | Words (of ~2,400) | Tag slots |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **S1. Open in motion** | Drop mid-action with a concrete, legible WANT already running. No throat-clearing, no scene-setting paragraph. (C2 opens *on the egg already cracking*.) | 200–350 | `[LOOP:_ advance]` carried in from last chapter |
| **S2. Escalating want vs. obstacle** | The want meets a hardening obstacle; Liora applies competence; the obstacle answers back **harder**. 2–3 rising tries, each costing more. Stats/skills drawn only from STATSPINE. | 700–950 | `[IDLE-TICK]` lands inside one try |
| **S3. The "yes-but / no-and" turn** | The hinge. She gets it — *but* it triggers a worse thing (yes-but); OR she fails — *and* the situation deteriorates (no-and). Never a clean win, never a flat loss. **THE COOKIE LANDS ON THE TURN** so the satisfaction and the complication arrive in the same beat. | 250–400 | **`[COOKIE:competence/number-up/mystery]` HERE** · `[LOOP:_ advance/close]` |
| **S4. Disaster or cost** | The turn's price is paid on-page and felt: a stat hit, a new upkeep, an ache, a deadline shortened. Cozy floor holds (G1) — the cost targets home/way-of-life/effort, never a loved creature's safety. | 250–400 | upkeep booked (G3) |
| **S5. Short sequel** | The de-escalation valve, kept *short* so momentum survives. Three sub-beats: **(a) reaction** (Liora's dry, brief emotional register — not a wallow), **(b) dilemma** (the new question the disaster posed, stated legibly per G2), **(c) new decision** that fires the next chapter. | 450–650 | `[LOOP:_ open]` (the new door) |
| **S6. Hook-out** | End on the decision's edge or a System soft-hook note that *implies* the next problem. Resolved-then-bigger (G6): this chapter's own promise is paid in S3–S4; the hook is appetite, not a fall. | 100–200 | **`[OUT:hook]`** · door cracked |

**Budget note:** S2 is the engine — give it the most room. S5 is deliberately lean; a
long sequel is where a tension chapter goes slack. If over 2,800, cut from S2's third try,
never from the S3 turn (that's where the cookie lives).

### 1.2 — VOICE GUARD (Scene&Sequel)

**Rule: disaster and reaction are the two beats most likely to swell into lyric. They may
not.** Tenderness and stakes stay inside Liora's dry deadpan register (CANON voice
fingerprint #1, #5). When S4 (cost) or S5a (reaction) tempts a lyrical/literary sentence —
the underline-me sentence, the metaphor reaching for the sublime — **redirect to deadpan**
via one of the four canon moves:

1. **Dry deflection over a fixed core** — *"No." landed softly; everyone heard it anyway.*
   (C1) Feel the weight; say it flat.
2. **Bureaucracy-as-metaphor** — *"Panic is just urgency wearing noisy shoes."* / *"standing
   had become unnecessary paperwork."* (C1/C2) Route emotion through the clerk's lexicon.
3. **System speaks the tenderness so Liora doesn't have to** — the Custodian Note carries
   the theme (*"Care is structural." / "Heat is not harm when given somewhere safe to go."*
   C1/C2), in deadpan customer-service with an editorial sting.
4. **Staccato one-beat lines for the spike** — *"Knock. / Knock. / Crack."* (C2) Cut, don't
   pour.

**Hard fail (Lane Sentinel returns `LITERARY-CONTAMINATED`):** a sentence that asks to be
underlined instead of read through (G2); interiority that stalls the clock; the two C1
lyrical lines reproduced or imitated (CANON voice-rule #5 — the only canon drift, do not
extend it). **Tenderness IN the register, never ABOUT it.**

### 1.3 — Worked mini-example (cast: **Cinder** the Ember-Toad; loop `L-CINDER`)

*A late-Arc-2 placement-pressure chapter. Clock: a forge-patron is interviewing Cinder as
a match (ARCH 2D.1 `L-CINDER` marker "Ep16 forge-patron appears"). Foreground axis ④ Social.*

- **S1 (open in motion):** The forge-patron is already in the Tea Room, already unimpressed,
  already saying the word "liability." Cinder is in the basin, sparking on cue at exactly the
  wrong moment. WANT: prove fire-isn't-harm to a man who buys his confidence wholesale.
  `[LOOP:L-CINDER advance]`
- **S2 (want vs. obstacle):** Liora runs the demonstration — controlled basking, the cooling
  ring, the appetite economy. Each time Cinder behaves, the patron raises the bar; each raise
  costs Liora more Hearthglow to keep the ring stable. `[IDLE-TICK]` System posts a quiet
  Hearthglow drain line mid-demo. Third try: the patron pokes the basin to test it.
- **S3 (yes-but turn + COOKIE):** Cinder *passes* — eats the ash-meal, banks the spark, stays
  warm-not-wild — **but** the poke startles him and he combusts a corner of the patron's order
  ledger. **`[COOKIE:competence-display]`** (the care-method visibly worked) lands *on the same
  beat* as the new mess. `[LOOP:L-CINDER advance]`
- **S4 (cost):** The patron walks. Liora books the upkeep: a perfect match seeded but not
  closed; Cinder is now a creature the cast can't bear to send anyway (the ARCH 2D.1 carry —
  Cinder's send-off defers to Book 2). Cost felt, cozy floor intact (nobody harmed).
  `[VOICE-GUARD]` — resist *"the ash settled like the end of something"*; write instead:
  *"The patron left. Cinder ate the apology."*
- **S5 (sequel):** (a) reaction — Liora, dry: *"We're zero for one. He's two for breakfast."*
  (b) dilemma — if the best match won't take him, is keeping him a failure of the pipeline or
  the start of the family? (c) decision — run the pipeline on the *next* foster instead; let
  Cinder stay. `[LOOP:L-PLACE open/advance]` (the door this cracks)
- **S6 (hook-out):** System soft-hook: *"Note: placement deferred. Reason on file: emotional
  liability. Status: acceptable."* **`[OUT:hook]`** — the next foster's clock is now armed.

## 2 — KISHŌTENKETSU TEMPLATE  (the ~60% cozy chapters — the book's dominant mode)

> Use for: care chapters, habitat/process chapters, the literary-ASMR zone, cast-bonding
> beats, the Lull-class summit chapters. **Conflict is NOT the engine** — recontextualization
> is. Satisfies without manufactured antagonism (3.3). Out = **warm** (never three warm-outs
> in a row, G6 — CADENCE.md spaces them).

### 2.1 — Beat skeleton (word budget for a 2,000–2,800-word episode)

| # | Beat | What it does | Words (of ~2,400) | Tag slots |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **K1. KI — cozy status-quo + a small want** | Establish warmth and a *modest, legible* want (a habitat to mend, a tea to steep right, a feeding to land). No threat. The want is small enough to feel safe and real enough to pull. (C1's moonwell, C2's basin — domestic, concrete.) | 300–450 | `[IDLE-TICK]` clock/mystery ticks quietly so G5 holds even here |
| **K2. SHŌ — gentle development / process (the ASMR zone)** | The longest, slowest beat: the *process* rendered with loving specificity — the steps, the textures, the inventory, the creatures' small business. This is the literary-ASMR the genre is bought for. No rising antagonist; the pleasure is competence-at-rest and detail. | 850–1,150 | care-method shown (axis ③); Bond/Comfort nudge |
| **K3. TEN — the unexpected, non-threatening turn** | The pivot unique to this form: **something appears that recontextualizes K1–K2 without endangering anything.** A creature does an unforeseen kind thing; a cleaned surface reveals a clue; the System softens a degree; a synergy fires that no one engineered. Surprise, not threat. **THE COOKIE LANDS IN THE TEN.** | 350–550 | **`[COOKIE:mystery/relationship/joke]` HERE** · `[LOOP:_ advance/clue]` |
| **K4. KETSU — warm reconciliation + quiet progress** | The *ten* is absorbed into the home; the chapter's small want is met *and now means more* than it did in K1. A stat/Comfort/Bond gain books the "quiet progress." The home is safe-for-now, warmly. | 300–500 | number/Bond/Comfort up; upkeep still booked (G3) |
| **K5. Warm-out** | End on a cozy floor beat — a synergy-pair tableau (Kraken↔Cress corner, Cinder↔Cress calm) or a deadpan System note that closes warm and *implies* (softly) the next small thing. Door cracked at warm size. | 150–250 | **`[OUT:warm]`** · small door cracked |

**Budget note:** SHŌ (K2) is the heart — protect its length; it is where the cozy-dominance
lives. The TEN (K3) must stay a *turn*, not a *threat* — if it raises a clock, you've drifted
into Scene&Sequel; re-classify the chapter or soften the turn. KETSU is short and warm.

### 2.2 — VOICE GUARD (Kishōtenketsu)

**Rule: the SHŌ (ASMR process) and the TEN (recontextualizing turn) are where the prose
most wants to go lyrical — the underline-me sentence about moonlight, memory, the sublime.
It may not.** The cozy mode earns its warmth through **specific, dry, slightly-wry detail**,
not lyricism (G2; CANON voice-rule #5). The Lull and all resonance are rendered in the dry
register — *inventory, three snores in three keys* — never lyricized (ARCH 2C.2). When a beat
reaches for lyric, redirect to deadpan:

- **Specificity replaces lyricism** — not *"the tea smelled of comfort itself"* but
  *"the tea smelled of porridge, rain, and the moment just before falling asleep"* (C1 —
  concrete triad, last item wry). Three concrete nouns beat one abstract feeling.
- **Let the System hold the theme** — the Custodian Note delivers tenderness in deadpan so
  the prose doesn't strain (*"Disagree." / "Note: Thank you for choosing care."* C1).
- **Undercut the swell** — when warmth peaks, deflate by a half-step: *"Or dehydration. She
  would investigate after finding tea."* (C1) The deadpan is the tenderness's container.
- **Object-comedy over sentiment** — give the feeling to a prop (the offended teapot, the
  waistcoated spider, the ambitious stove) rather than a lyrical narrator.

**Hard fail (`LITERARY-CONTAMINATED`):** ambiguity-as-virtue; a withheld meaning standing in
for a payoff (G2); interiority that stalls the read; any imitation of the two C1 lyrical
lines. **The TEN is felt, then named flatly** — Liora notices the beautiful thing and says
something dry about it. That is the register's whole trick.

### 2.3 — Worked mini-example (cast: **Tea Kraken** + **Cress**; loop `L-KRAKEN`)

*An Arc-1/2-hinge cozy chapter (the signature ASMR corner per ARCH 2C.1, SM Kraken↔Cress
synergy). Foreground axis ③ Texture. Carries the Arc-1 mystery button: the tea softens the
System — first evidence the voice is alive (ARCH 2C.2 Wave-1 button, ~Ep11–12).*

- **K1 (ki + small want):** Morning at Briarwick. The Brew Pool steams; the want is small and
  legible — get the house tea *right* for once so the morning lands. No threat. `[IDLE-TICK]`
  System posts the day's Comfort number, unbothered.
- **K2 (shō — ASMR process):** The full steep, rendered in dry specific detail: the Kraken
  lowering the leaves, the water going from brown to cloudy to clear (echo of C1's moonwell
  pump), Cress drifting close, the inventory of mugs (the sleeping-tortoise teapot, the three
  cracked ones). Competence-at-rest. Bond/Comfort nudge up quietly. No antagonist.
- **K3 (ten — turn + COOKIE):** The steep lands *perfectly* — and as the cup is poured, the
  System's deadpan **drifts a half-degree warmer**: a Custodian Note that's too specific, too
  *fond*, to be generic. **`[COOKIE:mystery-clue]`** lands here — first on-page evidence the
  voice is *alive* to care, that someone is in there. Non-threatening, recontextualizing: the
  whole house tea ritual was, all along, a way of *talking back to someone*. `[LOOP:L-KRAKEN
  advance]` · feeds `L-EDDA` (idle).
- **K4 (ketsu — warm reconciliation + quiet progress):** Liora doesn't solve the mystery; she
  pours a second cup and sets it where Cress and the Kraken settle together. Comfort up; the
  small want (good tea) is met and now means *more* — the tea is a conversation. `[VOICE-GUARD]`
  — resist *"the steam rose like a held breath finally let go"*; write *"The cup steamed. The
  System said something almost kind, then caught itself."*
- **K5 (warm-out):** Kraken↔Cress synergy tableau in the dry register — *two creatures and one
  cup, none of them in a hurry.* System soft note implies (gently) the next small thing.
  **`[OUT:warm]`** — door cracked at warm size (the voice has a person behind it; who?).

## 3 — HOW A CHAPTER SIGNALS ITS TEMPLATE (keeping cadence tags legible)

The two templates must be **distinguishable at a glance** so CADENCE.md's tags, the Sentinels,
and the beat-generators never disagree about what a chapter is. The signal is **structural,
not labelled in prose** — the reader never sees the tag; the engine always does.

**The one-line tells (any TWO confirm the type):**

| Signal | SCENE&SEQUEL | KISHŌTENKETSU |
|---|---|---|
| **The turn** | yes-but / no-and (win-with-cost or loss-with-deterioration) | *ten* (non-threatening recontextualization; no antagonist) |
| **Cookie location** | on the S3 turn | in the K3 *ten* |
| **Out** | `[OUT:hook]` (soft or hard) | `[OUT:warm]` |
| **Live clock** | a clock is foreground (inspection/spike/placement) | clock is idle-tick only (background) |
| **Closing cookie types it favors** | competence-display · number-up | relationship · mystery-clue · landed joke |

**The machine-readable header (the load-bearing signal).** Every generated chapter opens with
a one-line structural header the prose layer strips before publication:

```
<!-- MICRO: SCENE&SEQUEL | cookie:competence@turn | out:hard-hook | clock:L-INSPECT | loops: +L-PLACE ~L-CINDER -none -->
<!-- MICRO: KISHOTENKETSU | cookie:mystery@ten | out:warm | clock:idle | loops: ~L-KRAKEN ~L-EDDA -none -->
```

Fields: `MICRO:` template name · `cookie:` type@location (must read `@turn` for S&S, `@ten`
for K) · `out:` warm / soft-hook / hard-hook · `clock:` the foreground loop ID, or `idle` for
Kishōtenketsu · `loops:` `+`opens / `~`advances / `-`closes.

**Legibility rule (the consistency check the Sentinels run):**
1. `out:warm` REQUIRES `MICRO:KISHOTENKETSU` and `cookie:…@ten`. A warm-out on a Scene&Sequel
   header is malformed — reject.
2. `out:hook` REQUIRES `MICRO:SCENE&SEQUEL` and `cookie:…@turn`. A hook on a Kishōtenketsu
   header is malformed — reject.
3. `clock:idle` is legal ONLY for Kishōtenketsu; any S&S chapter must name a foreground loop.
4. The header's `cookie:` type must match the cookie CADENCE.md scheduled for that chapter,
   and must differ from the adjacent chapters' (G4). Mismatch blocks the write.
5. **Every arc-climax header must read `MICRO:SCENE&SEQUEL`** (3.3). A Kishōtenketsu climax is
   malformed — reject.

Because the cookie's *location* is fused to the template (turn vs. ten) and the out-type is
fused to both, a single glance at the header tells the whole micro-shape — and any drift
(a warm-out hung on a tension skeleton, a clock smuggled into a cozy chapter) shows up as a
contradiction in the header before a single beat is written.
