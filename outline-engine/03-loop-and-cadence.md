# STAGE 3 — STRUCTURAL-LAYER ENGINEERING (the crown jewels)

*Five specialists, IN PARALLEL, against the locked `ARCHITECTURE.md`. Each owns one
structural layer. Engineering these *separately and explicitly* — instead of
hand-waving them inside prose beats — is what produces top-tier macro/micro
structure, threaded loops, and pacing. Outputs are tables, not paragraphs.*

---

## 3.1 — MACRO-LOOP ARCHITECT → contributes to `LOOPMAP.md`

> You design the book on the **nested Promise / Progress / Payoff** model.
> Define four levels of door:
> - **Series door** (opens Book 1, pays in a later book) — stated, left ajar.
> - **Book door** (the finish-compulsion) — opens Ch1, pays at the finale.
> - **Four arc doors** — each arc *closes its own promise AND opens a bigger one*
>   ("full satisfaction + the world just got bigger"). Never a cruel cliffhanger.
> - **Chapter doors** — handled by Cadence.
> For each door give: the **promise** (what it swears), the **progress markers**
> (the visible mid-points that prove movement), and the **payoff** (where/how it
> lands). Map each arc climax as the arc-door payoff that doubles as the next arc's
> hook. Output the four-level door table.

## 3.2 — THREADED-LOOP WEAVER → owns `LOOPMAP.md` (the centerpiece)

> You implement **MICE + LIFO loop nesting** (open A→B→C, close C→B→A). A reader's
> sense of "tight plotting" is *literally* loops closing in the reverse order they
> opened. Build the master loop table for all 49 chapters:
>
> | Loop ID | Type (M/I/C/E) | Promise (the question raised) | Opens (ch) | Closes (ch) | Nest depth | Pays cookie? |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
>
> Rules:
> - **MICE types:** Milieu (place/exploration — e.g. locked East Wing), Inquiry
>   (mystery — why care was outlawed), Character (internal — Liora→Matron), Event
>   (external — the 7-day inspection, Pike's war).
> - **LIFO discipline:** a loop opened later, inside another, must close *first*.
>   The biggest loops (series Inquiry, Liora's Character arc) open earliest and
>   close latest. Verify no loop closes before it opens and none dangles past the
>   book unless it is the deliberate series door.
> - **Density target:** 2–4 loops live at any moment — enough to braid, not so many
>   the reader loses the thread. Flag any chapter where >5 are open (overload) or
>   0 short-term loops are live (slack).
> - Every loop close must coincide with a cookie. Output the full table + a
>   "nesting diagram" showing the matryoshka order.

## 3.3 — CADENCE DESIGNER → owns `CADENCE.md`

> You schedule the **per-chapter rhythm** for all 49 chapters. For each chapter:
>
> | Ch | Cookie type | Out (hook/warm) | Live clock active | Micro-structure | Upgrade beat? |
> |---|---|---|---|---|---|
>
> Rules:
> - **Cookie law:** every chapter pays ≥1 cookie; vary the type so no two adjacent
>   chapters pay the same kind.
> - **Out alternation:** alternate hook-outs (tension) and warm-outs (comfort) so
>   the rhythm breathes; never two cruel hooks in a row, never three warm-outs (sag).
> - **Upgrade cadence:** a larger sanctuary/progression upgrade every ~3 chapters.
> - **Anti-plateau:** confirm a live clock or deepening mystery is checked "active"
>   in every chapter of every arc; flag any run of 2+ with none.
> - **Micro-structure assignment (the secret weapon):** tag each chapter
>   **SCENE&SEQUEL** (for tension chapters — goal/conflict/disaster + reaction/
>   dilemma/decision) or **KISHŌTENKETSU** (for cozy/warm chapters — ki/development/
>   *ten* gentle twist/reconciliation, which satisfies WITHOUT manufactured conflict).
>   Target roughly 60% Kishōtenketsu / 40% Scene&Sequel to hold cozy dominance while
>   keeping momentum — this is how the book stays warm without going slack or going
>   grimdark. Every arc climax is Scene&Sequel.

## 3.4 — MICRO-STRUCTURE DESIGNER → contributes the chapter templates

> You write the two reusable chapter blueprints the beat-generators will fill:
> - **SCENE&SEQUEL template:** open in motion → escalating want vs obstacle →
>   "yes-but / no-and" turn → disaster or cost → short sequel (reaction, dilemma,
>   new decision that fires the next chapter). Cookie placed at the turn; hook-out.
> - **KISHŌTENKETSU template:** ki (cozy status-quo with a small want) → shō
>   (gentle development/process — the literary-ASMR zone) → ten (an unexpected but
>   non-threatening turn that recontextualizes) → ketsu (warm reconciliation +
>   quiet progress). Cookie in the *ten*; warm-out.
> Give each template a "voice guard" line: keep tenderness inside Liora's dry
> register; if a beat wants a lyrical/literary sentence, redirect to deadpan.

## 3.5 — PROGRESSION-SPINE KEEPER → owns `STATSPINE.md`

> You enforce the **binding stat contract**. Produce the strict unlock ledger:
> every skill/stat/feature, the exact chapter it unlocks, and the proportionate
> **new upkeep/problem** it introduces (anti power-creep). Then give each arc's
> **entry-state and exit-state** stat block (Comfort, Compliance %, per-creature
> Bond Trust, Hearthglow, Rank, Renown). Rule: nothing may win a climax that does
> not appear here earlier, in sequence. The beat-generators must not invent an
> ability — they draw only from this ledger. Flag any architecture beat that needs
> an unlock not yet on the spine.
