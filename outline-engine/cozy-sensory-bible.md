# COZY SENSORY & ATMOSPHERE BIBLE — Book 1

*The engineered "world-feeling" layer the prose stage draws from. Nothing here is
décor: every element is assigned a season, a room/creature, and (for the marquee
beats) an episode — so the cozy texture is scheduled, varied, and never a word-dump.
Pairs with `rooms-bible.md` (space), `cast-synergy-map.md` (chemistry), and
`build/CADENCE.md` (rhythm). Wonder = Studio-Ghibli natural awe; Resonance = the
Edda grief-engine already in the spine. This layer supplies the Ghibli half.*

## THE SEASONAL THROUGHLINE (the spine of the cozy layer)

Book 1 runs **late winter → high summer** — one rebirth arc (~5–6 months) that maps
season onto the emotional curve: the condemned, frozen ruin literally *thaws into
life* as Liora's care takes hold. Season is a free, always-on clock that deepens the
read without manufacturing conflict.

| Arc | Eps | Season | The feeling | Signature weather/light |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Seven-Day Trial** | 1–12 | **Late winter → first thaw** | warmth vs. cold; the ruin wakes | rain on the panes, **crackling hearth**, candlelight, frost, the first snowdrop through it, snowmelt beginning |
| **2 — Placement Pipeline** | 13–24 | **Spring** | new life, going out into the world | blossom, fresh wet green, **fuzzy chunky bees** waking, the brook running full, longer gold light |
| **3 — The Mystery Blooms** | 25–37 | **Late spring → midsummer** | lush abundance; the peak | **sunrays through leaf-canopy**, long grass on the hills, **fireflies** begin, warm dusk, **stars + the aurora night** |
| **4 — Recognition** | 38–49 | **High summer** | golden triumph, one storm, clear dawn | big **white fluffy clouds**, blue skies, **a summer thunderstorm** at the Pike climax breaking into clear **sunrise**, a **falling star** finale |

## NEW ROOM — THE BOOK NOOK (add to rooms-bible)

A window-seat reading corner: quilts, lamplight, a shelf of the nursery's old
records-turned-storybooks, rain on the glass. **Function:** the nightly *story hour*
where the scariest creatures (Cockatrice, Shriekmoth) are gentled by being read to
(Soothing Song engine). **Palette:** soft wool, warm low lamp, rain-on-glass, paper.
**Unlock:** ~Ep23 (Arc 2). **Cookie:** relationship/warm. The cozy-ASMR keystone the
critique said the Lull was carrying alone.

## MARQUEE WONDER SET-PIECES (scheduled, not just available)

Five Ghibli-grade beats placed on the cadence so wonder *escalates* with the seasons:

1. **Ep ~7 — First Thaw Rain** *(Arc 1 ASMR beat, already added)* — rain on the ruin,
   the hearth catches, the house holds its first warm night. Cozy floor established.
2. **Ep ~16 — The Brook Runs** *(Arc 2)* — snowmelt fills the courseway; the restored
   fountain runs clear; bees wake in the first blossom. Spring arrives *because the
   nursery is alive again*.
3. **Ep ~28 — The Lull, Firefly Dusk** *(Arc 3)* — the communal-sleep summit under the
   first fireflies; soft textiles, three snores in three keys, warm dark.
4. **Ep ~31–34 — The Sporeglow Aurora** *(Arc 3 peak)* — the West Wing lichen blooms
   into a slow indoor aurora; the family watches in the dark. Wonder fused to the
   mystery (the same room is Edda's secret). The book's visual high.
5. **Ep ~49 — Storm-Break Sunrise + Falling Star** *(Arc 4 finale)* — the summer
   thunderstorm (Pike) breaks into clear dawn under big clouds and blue sky; a falling
   star over the Moonwell; a wish; the nursery Recognized as Real. Warm-out, door ajar.

## SENSORY PALETTE PER SEASON (on-tap texture for prose drafting)

- **Late winter (Arc 1):** woodsmoke, rain-on-slate, candle-tallow, frost-bite, the
  *snap* of kindling, breath-fog, the first green snowdrop. Sound: rain, fire-crackle.
- **Spring (Arc 2):** wet earth, blossom, sap, bee-drone, brook-rush, lengthening gold
  light. Sound: birdsong, water, drowsy bees.
- **Midsummer (Arc 3):** cut grass, warm stone, leaf-canopy shade, firefly-glow,
  sporeglow, night air. Sound: leaves like surf, crickets, the hush of the Lull.
- **High summer (Arc 4):** sun-baked path, thunder-petrichor, cocoa, hay, the cool
  after-storm. Sound: thunder, then dripping eaves, then dawn-quiet.

**Voice guard (still binding):** render all of this in Liora's dry register — sensory
specificity, not lyrical swoon. "Rain on the glass; the fire had opinions about the
draught" — not "the rain wept silver down the ancient panes." The wonder lands
*because* the voice stays deadpan around it.
