# BRIARWICK CAST — SYNERGY & TENSION MAP

*Companion to `assets/briarwick-synergy-map.png`. The chemistry engine for the nine
creatures — designed so every pairing is a reusable comedy/heart beat, not just a
roster. **Synergy** = cozy chemistry. **Frenemy** = works brilliantly or backfires
(a built-in tension dial). **Anti-synergy** = comic friction the writer can mine on
any given chapter.*

## The cast

**Core found-family (permanent — green nodes):**
Tea Kraken · Corehound (3 toy heads) · Lunar Mooncalf (Cress) · Blink Gargoyle
(Bertram) · Cockatrice Chick.

**Fostered → matched to patrons (blue nodes):**
Pocket Phoenix · Ember-Toad (Cinder) · Puddle Slime · Shriekmoth (Kettle).

## The design rule

Every relationship is built on an **elemental/temperament axis** so the friction is
*legible and repeatable*: fire (Toad, Phoenix) vs water (Kraken, Slime); light vs
the light-allergic (everything glowy vs Shriekmoth); stone-calm (Gargoyle,
Cockatrice) vs anxiety (Phoenix); order vs the Corehound's three-way civil war. A
reader instantly understands *why* two creatures clash or click — which makes the
jokes land and the warmth earn out.

## Synergies (cozy chemistry)

| Pair | The beat |
|---|---|
| Ember-Toad ↔ Pocket Phoenix | A steady ember is the calmest thing in the building; the panic-bird finally stops self-combusting next to it. |
| Tea Kraken ↔ Lunar Mooncalf | Warm tea + cool moonlight = the single coziest corner in Briarwick; the ASMR set-piece. |
| Blink Gargoyle ↔ Cockatrice Chick | Both are basically rock. The cockatrice's death-glare does nothing; the gargoyle respects the effort. Two unbothered stones. |
| Cockatrice ↔ Puddle Slime | The cockatrice's failed-petrification pebbles get laid as paths that *corral the slime's puddles* — two problems solving each other. |
| Lunar Mooncalf ↔ Pocket Phoenix | Moon-calm talks the phoenix down off the ledge; fragility caring for anxiety. |
| Corehound ↔ Shriekmoth | Alarm-head + light-shriek = an elite (and deeply unpleasant) security system. |

## Frenemy (works… or backfires — the tension dial)

| Pair | The beat |
|---|---|
| Ember-Toad ↔ Tea Kraken | Toad heats the brew pool to a *perfect* steep — or overshoots into scalding, bitter, house-wide tea catastrophe. The same relationship is the win and the disaster. |

## Anti-synergies (comic friction)

| Pair | The beat |
|---|---|
| Ember-Toad ↔ Puddle Slime | Fire vs water, forever: the toad evaporates the slime's puddles, the slime douses the toad. Eternal low-stakes standoff. |
| Shriekmoth ↔ Lunar Mooncalf | The calf *glows* → the moth shrieks → the coziest room is instantly ruined. |
| Shriekmoth ↔ Pocket Phoenix | The phoenix flares → the moth shrieks → the shriek panics the phoenix → it bursts into ash. A feedback loop of mutual disaster. |
| Puddle Slime ↔ Blink Gargoyle | The gargoyle blinks across the room and lands squarely in a fresh puddle. "Mm." |
| Cockatrice ↔ Pocket Phoenix | The failed petrification still *looks* terrifying — enough to spook the phoenix into a small explosion. |
| Tea Kraken ↔ Corehound | A tentacle surfaces in the pot and steals the biscuit; the Corehound's food-head is *outraged* (the other two heads don't care). |
| Corehound ↔ itself | Three heads, three thermostats, zero agreement — the running internal civil war. |

## How to use it

- **Per-chapter cookie:** any anti-synergy edge is a guaranteed landed-joke on demand.
- **Warm-out beats:** any synergy edge is a ready-made cozy chapter-ender.
- **Escalation dial:** the frenemy edge (Toad↔Kraken) is the one to push when an arc
  needs gentle rising pressure without breaking cozy.
- **The Shriekmoth is the hub of friction** (it clashes with everything luminous), so
  its placement/adoption arc carries the most comic mileage — and its send-off is a
  relief *and* a small heartbreak.
